## LinkedIn (EN)

On June 9, Korea enacted its Research Data Act. For the first time, data produced by national R&D projects falls under a clear legal regime: institutions hold defined rights and management duties, openness becomes the default, and a national platform makes the data discoverable. Korea now stands where the US, with its Genesis Mission, and the EU, through Horizon Europe and FAIR, already do — treating research data as core infrastructure for the AI era.

But one distance remains. Opening data and making it usable are not the same problem. Across empirical studies, the share of research data that is findable approaches 100%, while the share that is actually reusable hovers near half. Without metadata, provenance, and quality, open data never becomes an input for reproduction or retraining.

The year before the law takes effect is not time for paperwork. It is time to build a data foundation that can actually be used.

## Facebook (EN)

A scientist re-runs the same measurement on the same instrument.

The data already exists somewhere. An earlier researcher produced it. But it cannot be found.

Korea's Research Data Act, promulgated on June 9, is a law aimed at this kind of repetition. It assigns clear responsibility for managing data that comes out of national R&D, makes openness the default, and lets researchers locate data through a shared platform.

But while reading through the material, one contrast kept holding my attention.

The share of research data that is findable reaches nearly 100%, while the share that is actually reusable settles around half.

"Is open data really data the next person can use?"

Data uploaded without metadata, without provenance, without quality checks stays open and still never becomes an input for reproduction or retraining. Opening is the starting line, not the finish.

For the next researcher, and for an AI system, to actually use it, one more layer is needed: the quality, metadata, and provenance that make data AI-Ready. That is where Pebblous, having spent years quantifying data quality, finds this law worth watching.

Whether the year before the law takes effect ends as paperwork, or becomes the time to build a foundation that can truly be used, depends on how we close the distance between open and usable. The value of the research data we have already paid for rests there.
