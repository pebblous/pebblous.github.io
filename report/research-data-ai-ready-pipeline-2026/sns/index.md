## LinkedIn (EN)

94.7% of researchers say they know the FAIR principles. Only 11% of repositories actually meet all of them for their own data.

That gap between knowing and doing is why depositing data and having that data feed an AI model are two different things. Uploading to a repository doesn't make data trainable. In between sits a process: putting an ML-native metadata standard (Croissant, already adopted by 700,000+ datasets) on top of discovery metadata, measuring quality against ISO/IEC 5259, and recording lineage and rights.

What happens when quality goes unmeasured? Even the test sets of major ML benchmarks carried an average of 3.3% mislabeled examples, and correcting them reshuffled the model rankings.

With Korea's National Research Data Act taking effect in May 2027, the national repository DataON has automated only the discovery layer, leaving four gaps: automated validation, ML metadata, quality measurement, and curation. The law has passed. The open question is how to automate this process at a scale humans can sustain. Pebblous runs DataClinic to diagnose exactly these defects in industrial data every day.

Depositing data and having a model actually learn from it turn out to be far apart. In between, a machine has to find the data, read it, measure its quality, and trace where it came from. When that process doesn't run on its own, even carefully archived data quietly becomes data no one can use.

How much does unmeasured quality cost? Even the benchmarks we trusted carried an average of 3.3% mislabeled examples, and fixing them reshuffled the rankings. When data pretends to be more certain than it is, the judgments built on top of it wobble too.

Having diagnosed the quality of industrial data every day, we find this blank space a familiar face.

In May 2027, Korea's National Research Data Act takes effect. The law has passed, and the question has moved somewhere more practical: who fills the process between deposit and learning, and at a scale people can actually sustain? If you've ever uploaded a dataset and wondered where it went, this is a question worth sitting with.
