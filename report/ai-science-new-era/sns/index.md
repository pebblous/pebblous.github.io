## LinkedIn (English)

💡 AI writes a research paper. It passes peer review. Then the team voluntarily withdraws it.

That's not a failure — it's actually the most honest moment in the current AI-in-science debate.

Here's what actually happened:

Sakana AI's AI Scientist v2 submitted three papers to the ICLR 2025 workshop "I Can't Believe It's Not Better." One passed peer review (score: 6.33/10). It was then voluntarily withdrawn — as pre-agreed with the workshop organizers.

A few things to keep straight:

▸ This was a *workshop* track (~32.6% acceptance rate), not the ICLR main track
▸ 1 of 3 papers was accepted. The other two scored 3s
▸ The paper that "passed" was a negative-results paper on compositional regularization
▸ The Nature 651 paper everyone cited? Written by *human* authors (Lu, C. et al.) — a system description, not an AI-generated paper

Meanwhile, JAIGP (Journal of AI-Generated Papers), launched February 2026 by economist César Hidalgo, is publishing AI-authored papers — 43 so far. No traditional academic recognition yet, but the experiment is real.

The more quietly alarming trend: hallucinated citations in papers jumped from 0.3% (2024) to 2.6% (2025) — a 9x increase in one year. AI-generated papers flowing into academic databases, then back into AI training data. A contamination loop already in motion.

The productivity math is seductive: $20–$25 to generate a paper vs. $3,500+ in publication costs for human researchers. But 2 of 3 AI-generated papers were rejected. Hallucinated citations. Repeated figures. Subtle misattributions.

Speed without verifiability is noise, not science.

As AI accelerates paper production, the value of data provenance and quality diagnostics rises in parallel. That's exactly what we're building with DataClinic — diagnostic layers that identify whether the data behind a model is trustworthy, regardless of who (or what) wrote the paper using it.

What's your read on this? Is peer review equipped to handle AI-generated submissions at scale?

## Facebook (한국어)

💡 AI가 논문을 쓴다고 하면, 얼마나 믿어야 할까요?

최근 두 가지 실험이 화제입니다.

하나는 JAIGP(Journal of AI-Generated Papers) — AI만 저자로 등재되는 저널입니다.
César Hidalgo 교수가 2026년 2월 론칭했고, 벌써 43편이 게재됐습니다.

다른 하나는 Sakana AI Scientist v2 — 가설 수립부터 논문 작성까지 전 과정을 AI가 자동으로 수행합니다.
논문 한 편 생성 비용은 약 $20~$25이죠.
인간 연구자의 출판 비용($3,500~$4,000+)과 비교하면 놀라운 격차입니다.

그런데 헤드라인과 현실 사이에는 거리가 있습니다.

"AI가 쓴 논문이 Nature에 게재됐다" — 사실이 아닙니다.
Nature 651에 실린 것은 인간 저자(Lu, C. et al.)가 AI Scientist 시스템을 소개한 논문입니다.

"ICLR 2025 피어리뷰 통과" — 워크숍 트랙입니다.
3편 제출 중 1편 수락, 이후 사전 합의에 따라 자진 철회됐습니다.

더 조용하지만 더 심각한 문제도 있습니다.

AI 생성 논문이 학술 데이터베이스에 유입되고, 그게 다시 AI 훈련 데이터가 되는 루프입니다.
환각 인용 논문 비율이 2024년 0.3%에서 2025년 2.6%로 단 1년 만에 9배 뛰었습니다.

논문의 양이 늘수록 품질을 검증하는 눈이 더 중요해지는 시대.
어떤 데이터를 신뢰할 수 있는지, 어떻게 판단하시겠어요?
