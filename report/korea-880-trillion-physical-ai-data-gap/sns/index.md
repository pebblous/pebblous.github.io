한국이 10년간 1,350조 원을 반도체·데이터센터·휴머노이드에 붓는다. 역사상 최대 규모다. 그런데 이 예산표에서 로봇이 몸으로 쌓을 경험 데이터에 붙은 몫은 전체의 약 0.1%다. 팹과 데이터센터 옆에서 사실상 소수점 이하로 줄어든다. 더 결정적인 건, 투자자와 정책 담당자가 실제로 읽는 세 외신 요약본에는 그 데이터 항목이 아예 없다는 점이다.

휴머노이드의 능력은 몸체가 아니라 그 로봇이 겪은 상호작용 데이터에서 나온다. Open X-Embodiment는 여러 로봇의 데이터를 합쳐 일반화 성능을 최대 3배 끌어올렸다. 그런데 이 데이터는 팹처럼 자본으로 지을 수도, 로봇처럼 돈으로 사 올 수도 없다. 접촉이 실제로 일어나야만 생기는 노동집약 자산이고, 표준 구조로 검증해야 비로소 재사용된다.

정부가 말한 '1만 시간'은 수집의 언어다. 검증되지 않은 데이터를 그냥 섞으면 로봇의 과제 도달률이 73%에서 43%로 무너진다. 정적 파일 청결도로는 보이지 않는 붕괴다. 이미 중국은 국가 표준화기구를 세웠고, 테슬라는 공장을, 엔비디아는 시뮬레이션을 데이터 파이프라인으로 만들었다. 20% 점유율의 진짜 승부처는 팹 개수가 아니라 예산표에 아직 긋지 않은 한 줄이다.

## LinkedIn (EN)

South Korea will spend $88 billion over ten years on chips, data centers, and humanoid robots. It is the country's largest-ever industrial plan. Yet the line for the experience data those robots must accumulate is roughly 0.1% of the total. Next to the fabs and data centers, it shrinks below a rounding error. More telling: the three international reports that investors and policymakers actually read do not mention the data item at all.

A humanoid's capability comes not from its body but from the interaction data it has lived through. Open X-Embodiment lifted generalization up to threefold simply by pooling data across robots. But this data can't be built like a fab or bought like a robot. It is a labor-intensive asset that exists only when real contact happens, and it becomes reusable only after it is validated against a standard structure.

The government's "10,000 hours" is the language of collection. Mix in unvalidated data and a robot's task success rate collapses from 73% to 43%, a failure invisible to any static file check. China has already stood up a national standards body; Tesla treats its factories as data pipelines; NVIDIA sells simulation as one. The real contest behind a 20% market share is not the number of fabs but a budget line Korea has yet to draw.

## Facebook (EN)

I once laid out the budget on a single page and read it for a long while.

800 trillion won for the semiconductor fabs. 550 trillion for the AI data centers. A target for mass-producing humanoids. Every line carries a name, an amount, a facility.

And then I looked for the one line that would belong to the experience a robot has to earn with its own body.

It is there, if you dig. About 1.4 trillion won, under the name "robot training center." Roughly 0.1% of the total. Open the international briefings the world actually reads, and that line simply isn't there.

A humanoid's ability does not come from its frame. Two robots with the same body diverge by what they have lived through. A fab gets built when you pour in capital; a robot arrives when you pay for it. But the experience a robot leaves behind, colliding with the world, is earned only by accumulating it over time or refining it against a standard. It cannot be bought, and it cannot be built. It can only be gathered and kept.

So I keep thinking that "how much we collect" and "what we can actually use" are two very different questions. Blend in unvalidated data and the file looks fine, yet the moment the robot moves, its task success falls from 73% to 43%. A collapse a static eye never sees. That is why Pebblous validates data in a closed simulation loop rather than by file cleanliness.

You can buy the fabs. You can buy the robots. Making the experience they gather trustworthy, though, may begin with a single line no one has drawn yet.
