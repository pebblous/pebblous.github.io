🚀 Two of MLflow's biggest competitors disappeared in under a year.

Neptune.ai was acquired by OpenAI and shut down March 5, 2026.
Weights & Biases was acquired by CoreWeave — shifting from an independent MLOps platform to a GPU cloud add-on.

The consolidation happened fast. And it made MLflow's position clearer than ever.

MLflow now stands as the only major MLOps platform that has retained open-source independence and data portability. The numbers back it up:

🔸 20,000+ GitHub Stars
🔸 33M+ monthly PyPI downloads
🔸 34.3% adoption in experiment tracking (2.5× W&B's 13.4%, per DataTalks.Club 2025)
🔸 Four major academic surveys (2022–2026) confirm it as the de facto standard

MLflow 3.0 isn't just an update — it's a strategic pivot to GenAI. LoggedModel shifts the paradigm from run-centric to model-centric lineage. Prompt Registry brings versioned prompt management. Built-in LLM Judge evaluation and production-scale OpenTelemetry tracing complete the picture.

The MLOps market is projected to grow from $2.3B in 2025 to $16.6B by 2030 (CAGR 40.5%). The tools that survive consolidation will be the ones that own the full observability stack — from data quality upstream to model evaluation downstream.
