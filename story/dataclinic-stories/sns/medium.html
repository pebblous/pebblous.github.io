The Stories Behind AI Dataset Numbers

What is the most ordinary bird? We diagnosed 525 species and the one AI picked as most typical was the peacock — not because it's showy, but because within that dataset it sat closest to the average. That is the kind of small, strange finding that DataClinic Diagnostic Stories are made of.

The series is a record of opening up real AI datasets, one at a time, and reading the story behind the quality score. ImageNet's 1.43 million images, 150 kinds of Korean food, defense synthetic data, 525 bird species — 134 datasets in all, roughly 12 million images, looked at through Pebblous's data imaging technology. Each story shows the score, the cluster distribution, and the representative and outlier samples, so a reader can see for themselves how AI "sees" a dataset.

The dataset that gave birth to deep learning

The natural place to start is ImageNet. Fei-Fei Li's 1.43-million-image dataset triggered the deep learning revolution, and revisiting it with DataClinic surfaces what a decade of use had smoothed over — label noise, duplicates, and points of class confusion. It's a reminder that even the field's most famous dataset is not the same thing as a clean one.

When a high score hides a weakness

The deepfake story is where the numbers get uncomfortable. A dataset of 191,859 images scored 91 — high quality by any headline reading. But watching the clusters shift from L2 triangles to L3 heart shapes reveals exactly where a detector trained on it could be fooled. A good score, it turns out, can be the place a problem hides rather than the proof there isn't one.

Perfect balance, mediocre score

The drone classification story runs the opposite way. Class balance is perfect and integrity is 100%, and yet the dataset lands at 76. The culprit is the video-frame trap — thousands of near-identical frames pulled from continuous footage, inflating counts without adding real variety. It's the clearest case for why "balanced" and "good" are not the same word.

Why this matters

AI model performance starts with data quality, and quality is rarely captured by a single number on a dashboard. These stories exist to make that visible — to show that behind every dataset statistic there is a shape, a bias, and a decision waiting to be understood. That's the work Pebblous does: diagnosing and raising the quality of the data AI learns from, one dataset at a time.
