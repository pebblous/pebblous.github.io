## LinkedIn (EN)

Elon Musk: "AI could be more dangerous than nuclear weapons." Geoffrey Hinton: "10-20% chance AI causes human extinction." Dario Amodei: "AI could create weapons of mass destruction." Larry Ellison: "AI will enable constant citizen surveillance."

These same individuals are simultaneously investing hundreds of billions in AI infrastructure. Senator Bernie Sanders confronts this contradiction head-on, pushing for an AI data center construction moratorium. Three years of warnings have accumulated, but regulation has not moved an inch. AI super PAC funding flowing into Congress is part of the reason.

Sanders' argument rests on three pillars: massive job displacement (Goldman Sachs: 300 million jobs affected), surveillance state scenarios (AI-powered real-time monitoring infrastructure), and existential risk (1,000 AI researchers' open letter calling for a 6-month moratorium). The bill is unlikely to pass, but the signal that serious AI governance debate has begun in US Congress matters.

Pebblous analyzes this issue from the perspective that AI governance and data quality are essential prerequisites for technological progress.
