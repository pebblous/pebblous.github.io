## LinkedIn (KO)

자기가 만든 데이터셋을 자기 진단 도구에 넣었더니 54점, "나쁨"이 나왔다. 페블러스가 실제로 한 일이다.

그 데이터셋은 DAL이 만든 Star-MNIST였고, 진단한 도구는 자사 DataClinic이었다. 10개 클래스의 평균 이미지가 모두 같은 회색 원으로 수렴하는 픽셀 공간의 함정을, 도구가 정직하게 짚어냈다. 데이터클리닉 허브는 이런 자가진단부터 국제표준 매핑, 특허, 파이프라인 자동화까지 이 제품의 모든 자리를 한데 모은다.

핵심 엔진 DataLens는 DNN에서 출발해 뉴로-심볼릭 아키텍처로 진화했고, Data Imaging 기술로 데이터셋의 품질 상태를 눈으로 볼 수 있게 만든다. 현대자동차, LG전자, LG유플러스, 한화비전과의 실증으로 검증됐고, ISO/IEC 5259가 요구하는 품질 측정 기준을 자동 산출하며, 2025년 조달청 혁신제품으로 지정됐다. 핵심 기술은 미국 특허 US 12,481,720 B2로 등록됐다.

균형 잡혀 보이던 MIT Places365 180만 장에서도 DataClinic은 61개 클래스가 피처 공간에서 겹친다는 걸 찾아냈다. 표면의 균형 아래 숨은 격차를 보는 일, 그게 데이터 품질이다.

## LinkedIn (EN)

We put our own dataset into our own diagnostic tool, and it came back scoring 54 — rated "Poor." That is a thing Pebblous actually did.

The dataset was Star-MNIST, built by our DAL team; the tool was DataClinic. The mean images of all ten classes converge to the same gray circle — a trap of pixel-space averaging — and the tool named it honestly. The DataClinic hub gathers every seat of this product, from that kind of self-diagnosis to international-standard mapping, patents, and pipeline automation.

The core engine, DataLens, evolved from a DNN architecture to a neuro-symbolic one, and its Data Imaging technology makes a dataset's quality state visible at a glance. The technology was validated through engagements with Hyundai Motor, LG Electronics, LG U+, and Hanwha Vision. It automatically computes the Quality Measures required by ISO/IEC 5259, was designated a Public Procurement Service Innovation Product in 2025, and its core method is registered as US patent US 12,481,720 B2.

Even in MIT's Places365 — 1.8M images that look perfectly balanced — DataClinic found 61 classes overlapping in feature space. Seeing the gap hidden beneath a balanced surface: that is what data quality is.

## Facebook (EN)

It isn't easy to call your own work bad with your own hands.

Pebblous tried it once.

We fed Star-MNIST, built by our DAL team, into our own DataClinic. It came back scoring 54, rated "Poor."

For a moment the number stung, but then it felt like a relief. It meant the tool had honestly caught that the mean images of all ten classes collapse into the same gray circle. A good diagnostic tool shouldn't only inspect other people's data; it has to keep its eyes open in front of its maker's data too.

Curious how far that honesty would go, I put a larger dataset through it. MIT's Places365 — 1.8 million images. On the surface it looked like the most balanced dataset imaginable. And yet DataClinic found 61 classes overlapping in feature space. Beneath a surface that seemed evenly divided, the visual identities were quietly blurring into one another.

"Data quality" often sounds abstract. But the work turned out to be this simple: standing in front of data that looks well-organized and asking, one more time, "is it really?" In the engagements with Hyundai Motor and LG Electronics, in the seat where it computes ISO 5259 measures automatically, what Pebblous has done with DataClinic was always the same.

What if there were one tool that looked at your own data honestly? I'd be glad if you'd carry that question with me.
