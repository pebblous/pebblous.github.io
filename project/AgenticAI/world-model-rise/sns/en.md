## LinkedIn (English)

🌱 Seeing a field and understanding a field are two very different things.

AI is facing the same question right now.

VLMs have eyes. They can describe what they see with remarkable accuracy.
VLAs have hands. They can translate instructions into physical action.

So why aren't they enough?

Because neither can answer this: *"If I do this now, what happens to the world in 5 seconds?"*

No causal reasoning.
No internal simulation.
No internalized physics.

A skilled farmer doesn't just look at a field — they model it.
They know how this soil responds to rain, how this seed behaves in cold, what the harvest will look like before a single seed is planted.
That's not perception. That's a world model.

This is the structural gap VLMs and VLAs share — and what world models are built to fill.

🔸 *V-JEPA 2 (Meta)* — learns physics in latent space, no pixel generation. Transfers to robot control with just 62 hours of robot data. 30× faster planning than Cosmos.

🔸 *NVIDIA Cosmos* — trained on 20M hours of real-world video. Generates physics-aware synthetic data for robotics and autonomous driving. 2M downloads in its first month.

🔸 *Genie 3 (DeepMind)* — generates interactive 3D environments at 24fps from a single text prompt. No physics engine required — it learned the rules from data.

🔸 *Dreamer 4 (DeepMind)* — solves long-horizon tasks like Minecraft's diamond challenge through imagination alone. The agent rehearses inside a mental simulation before acting.

Yann LeCun has been consistent: *"In 3–5 years, nobody will be using LLMs the way we use them today."* He then founded AMI Labs, raising €500M at a €3B valuation to back this bet directly.

At Pebblous, we see the same principle applying to data operations.

A DataGreenhouse pipeline that can only react to errors is like a VLA — capable, but blind to what comes next. The next step is pipelines that *simulate* the downstream impact of every data transformation before it runs.

Causal understanding isn't just a robotics problem. It's the foundation of truly autonomous systems — including autonomous data operations.
