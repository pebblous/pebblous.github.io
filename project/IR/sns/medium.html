Turning Data Value Into an Asset: Inside Pebblous's Investment Research Hub

In the Physical AI era, the question is no longer whether data matters. It is whether the value of data can be measured, priced, and treated as an asset the way a factory or a patent portfolio is. That question is easy to state and unusually hard to answer, and it sits at the center of how one Korean company frames its own investment story.

Pebblous, founded in November 2021, has spent its years on a single premise: data quality decides how well AI performs. The company has now collected the evidence for that premise into a single place, an investor-facing research hub, and the picture it assembles is worth reading closely.

What Pebblous is, in verified facts

Rather than a pitch, the hub reads as a record. The company reached KRW 3.1 billion in cumulative funding through a Pre-A round in October 2022, following its seed investment. Its product line, DataClinic, PebbloScope, and a synthetic-data solution, has been deployed alongside a set of references that are unusual for a company of its size:

  • Enterprise references: Hyundai Motor, LG Electronics, LG Uplus, and Hanwha Vision.
  • Public and defense trials: field proofs of concept with the Republic of Korea Army and Marine Corps.
  • National program: named a lead company in the Ministry of Science and ICT "Global Big Tech" program in 2025, bringing KRW 6 billion in support over three years.
  • Procurement: DataClinic designated an Innovative Product by Korea's Public Procurement Service in 2025.

The technical foundation is equally concrete. Pebblous holds a US-registered patent it calls "data value proof," and it runs AADS, an Agentic AI Data Scientist platform built on a neuro-symbolic engine. The moat, in other words, is an attempt to put a scale against something long treated as unpriceable.

What it is building next

Two systems are under construction. Data Greenhouse is an autonomous data operating system built on Agentic AI, extending DataClinic from diagnosis toward self-directed operation. PebbloSim is a synthetic-data generator for Physical AI, engineered to produce high-quality data without what the company terms "physical hallucination," the failure mode that appears when generated data violates the physical laws of the real world. The world Physical AI acts on is made of objects and forces, not sentences, and an error there is a different kind of error.

The market frame

The hub reads Pebblous against an external lens rather than its own optimism. It uses PitchBook's "2026 AI Outlook" framework, which sets out conditions for durable AI winners, and situates the company inside a data-management-software total addressable market that PitchBook sizes at $155.6 billion. The same section places Pebblous within the broader Sovereign AI trend, where nations and enterprises increasingly want data infrastructure they control.

On its own numbers, 2025 was a turnaround year. Revenue grew 115% year over year, the cost base fell by more than half, losses declined 81.5%, and the company secured KRW 1.7 billion in government R&D funding. These are reported results, not projections.

Reading the research, including what is provisional

The hub curates several documents: a shareholder letter from March 2026, the 2025 financial report, the PitchBook analysis, a Physical AI investment memo, and the patent portfolio. One item deserves careful reading. An internal investment review examines the ceiling of a $50M ARR argument and lays out four growth scenarios ranging from roughly KRW 10.8 billion to KRW 100 billion. These are scenarios the review outlines for discussion, not outcomes stated as fact, and several of the detailed IR materials require a password to view.

That distinction is the point. The hub does not promise a valuation or a return. It records what has happened, names what is still a scenario as a scenario, and leaves the reader to weigh the evidence. For a company whose entire thesis is that data value can be proven rather than asserted, that posture is the most consistent thing about it.
