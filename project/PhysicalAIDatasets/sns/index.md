## LinkedIn (KO)

로봇 데이터셋을 열어 보면 카메라 영상과 관절 상태, 행동 명령이 나란히 들어 있다. 정작 빠져 있는 것은 그 데이터가 어떻게 만들어졌는지의 이력이다.

로봇과 피지컬 AI가 학습하는 데이터를 한자리에 모은 허브를 열었다. 사람이 실제 로봇을 조종해 모은 원격조종 데이터(DROID·Open X-Embodiment)부터 시뮬레이터에서 물리 엔진으로 찍어낸 합성 데이터(GR00T·RoboCasa), 손끝의 압력과 미끄러짐을 담은 촉각 데이터, 그리고 데이터 품질과 표준까지 16편을 지도처럼 배치했다.

16편을 관통하는 한 가지 주제는 계통추적(provenance)이다. 대부분의 공개 데이터셋은 영상과 상태, 행동은 남기지만 접촉력과 생성 내력, 물리 일관성의 증명은 버린다. 데이터가 물리적으로 옳은지 사후에 확인할 방법이 사라지는 셈이다.

어디서부터 읽으면 좋을지 궁금하다면, 유명 로봇 데이터셋 6종을 스케일·라이선스·형식으로 비교한 지형도부터 보면 된다. 수집은 여섯 갈래로 갈라져도 배포는 LeRobot 형식 하나로 수렴하고, 여섯 모두 계통추적을 남기지 않는다는 공통점이 여기서 드러난다.

로봇/피지컬 AI 데이터에 관심이 있다면, 흩어져 있던 글들을 한 장의 지도로 모아 둔 이 허브가 시작점이 된다.

## LinkedIn (EN)

Open a robot dataset and you find camera frames, joint states, and action commands lined up side by side. What's missing is the record of how that data came to be.

We've opened a hub that gathers the datasets robots and physical AI learn from, all in one place. Teleoperation data collected by people driving real robots (DROID, Open X-Embodiment), synthetic data churned out by physics engines in simulators (GR00T, RoboCasa), tactile data capturing pressure and slip at a fingertip, and the questions of data quality and standards. Sixteen pieces, laid out like a map.

One theme runs through all sixteen: provenance. Most public datasets keep the frames, states, and actions but discard the proof — contact force, generation history, physical consistency. That leaves no way to verify, after the fact, whether the data is physically correct.

Not sure where to start? Begin with the landscape comparing six well-known robot datasets by scale, license, and format. Collection splits six ways, but distribution converges on a single LeRobot format, and none of the six record provenance — a pattern that becomes clear here.

If robot and physical AI data is on your radar, this hub pulls scattered writing into one map to read from.

## Facebook (EN)

When we say a robot learns something, what is it actually looking at?

Camera frames, joint angles, the action commands it was given. Open one public robot dataset and, more often than not, those three sit side by side.

What rarely survives is the record: the pressure at the fingertip when it touched an object, the moment it slipped just slightly, which lighting and which seed a demonstration was synthesized under.

Building a hub that gathers the data robots and physical AI learn from, we kept returning to one word — provenance. Whether the data was collected by a person driving a real robot or churned out by a physics engine in a simulator, most of it keeps only the result and discards how that result came to be. The data grows, but the evidence that the data is physically correct doesn't grow with it.

Sixteen pieces sit in this hub, laid out like a map — from teleoperation to tactile data, to labeling failed demonstrations, to data standards. They cover different subjects, but as you read, you notice they light up the same gap from different angles. Can data prove its own history? It's a question Pebblous has been holding onto for a long time at DataClinic.

If robot data has been on your mind, it might be worth reading these scattered stories in one place, side by side.
