## LinkedIn (KO)

7,000만 달러를 유치한 회사가 문을 닫고, 3.2억 달러짜리 회사는 NVIDIA에 팔렸다. 둘 다 합성데이터를 만드는 회사였다.

Datagen은 이미지 합성데이터 하나에 집중하다 자금을 다 태우고 폐업했다. Gretel은 품질 검증과 생성을 한 플랫폼에 묶었고, NVIDIA가 그 조각을 사 갔다. 같은 시장, 정반대의 결말. 갈림길은 데이터를 얼마나 많이 찍어내느냐가 아니라, 찍어낸 데이터를 믿을 수 있느냐였다.

왜 지금 이 시장이 뜨거운가. 로봇과 자율주행, 스마트팩토리가 학습할 물리 세계 데이터를 실제로 수집하는 건 느리고 비싸다. 그래서 디지털 트윈으로 만든 고정밀 합성데이터가 필수 인프라가 되고 있다. Gartner는 2030년까지 AI 학습 데이터의 60%가 합성데이터일 것으로 본다.

이 허브는 그 시장을 편별로 갈라 읽는다. 합성데이터 가격은 왜 모달리티마다 10배에서 100배씩 벌어지는지, 기업 여덟 곳이 어떻게 흥하고 망했는지, 디지털 트윈과 피지컬 AI 시장이 어디서 교차하는지, 그리고 생성보다 어려운 품질 검증을 누가 책임지는지. 페블러스는 데이터 진단에서 출발해 이 지점을 다룬다.

## LinkedIn (EN)

One company raised $70M and shut down. Another sold to NVIDIA for $320M. Both were in the business of making synthetic data.

Datagen bet everything on a single modality of image data, burned through its funding, and closed. Gretel tied quality validation and generation into one platform, and NVIDIA bought that piece. Same market, opposite endings. The fork was never about how much data you can print, but whether the printed data can be trusted.

Why is this market hot now. Collecting real-world data for robots, self-driving cars, and smart factories to learn from is slow and expensive. So high-fidelity synthetic data built on digital twins is becoming essential infrastructure. Gartner projects that by 2030, 60% of AI training data will be synthetic.

This hub reads that market chapter by chapter — why synthetic data pricing swings 10x to 100x across modalities, how eight companies rose and fell, where the digital twin and Physical AI markets cross, and who takes responsibility for the quality validation that is harder than generation itself. Pebblous starts from data diagnosis and works this exact point.

페블러스가 이 자리를 오래 들여다보는 이유도 거기에 있습니다. 데이터를 진단해 온 회사가 자연스럽게 도달한 좌표입니다. 만들기 전에 믿을 수 있는지부터 묻는 일. 데이터가 쏟아질수록 더 조용히, 더 근본적으로 필요해지는 일입니다.

## Facebook (EN)

A few days ago I paused while reading that a well-funded company had quietly closed its doors.

It was Datagen, which had raised $70M.

Around the same time, another synthetic data company, Gretel, sold to NVIDIA for $320M.

That two companies working the same raw material could end so differently stayed with me for a while. "What set these two apart?"

Looking for the answer, I found the posts I'd scattered over the past months had become a small pile. Why pricing swings so widely across modalities. How eight companies rose and fell. Where the digital twin and Physical AI markets meet. They sat in different places, but looking back, each was lighting up the same question from a different angle.

I've come to call that question "the threshold of trust." Making data in bulk is no longer the hard part. The hard part is vouching for whether that data holds the physics of the real world, whether it can be trusted for training. What separated the company that closed from the one that got bought was, in the end, this threshold.

That is why Pebblous keeps looking at this seat. It is the coordinate a company that has spent its years diagnosing data arrives at naturally. Asking whether something can be trusted before making it. As data pours out, the work grows quieter and more fundamental.

Data is already being made in abundance. Which of it can be trusted — I'd be glad if you'd think it through with me.
