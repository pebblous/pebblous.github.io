## LinkedIn (KO)

이 한 줄이 지난 몇 달 흩어져 쓰던 글을 한자리로 불러 모았다. AI가 학습하는 원료로만 취급되던 데이터가, 값을 매기고 소유권을 따지고 품질을 인증하는 자산으로 옮겨 가는 중이다. 자산이 되려면 세 가지가 필요하다. 값을 정하는 메커니즘, 품질을 재는 기준, 소유를 증명하는 체계.

이 허브는 그 세 축을 편별로 나눠 읽는다. Web3와 자기주권 신원으로 데이터 주권을 되찾는 이야기, 페블러스의 '가상환경 기반 데이터 가치 증명' 특허가 데이터에 가격표를 붙이는 방식, 12개 사업 영역과 87.2조 원 시장을 연 한국 디지털자산기본법, 그리고 ISO/IEC 5259의 QM 코드가 유사성과 대표성을 수학으로 재는 원리까지.

값과 소유권을 아무리 정교하게 설계해도, 그 아래 데이터가 믿을 만하지 않으면 시장은 서지 않는다. 데이터 자산화의 병목은 제도가 아니라 품질이다. 페블러스가 DataClinic으로 그 품질을 진단하는 이유도 여기에 있다.

## LinkedIn (EN)

The data-broker market is worth $319 billion, and the individual whose data it trades pockets $0.03.

That single line pulled together the posts I'd been writing separately over the past months. Data, long treated as raw fuel for AI to learn from, is shifting into an asset — one that gets priced, owned, and certified. Becoming an asset takes three things: a mechanism to set the price, a standard to measure quality, and a system to prove ownership.

This hub reads those three axes chapter by chapter. Reclaiming data sovereignty through Web3 and self-sovereign identity. How Pebblous's patent on virtual-environment data value proof puts a price tag on data. Korea's Digital Asset Basic Law, which opened 12 business areas and a KRW 87.2 trillion market for 11.13 million users. And the math inside ISO/IEC 5259, where QM codes measure similarity and representativeness.

However carefully you design price and ownership, the market won't stand if the data underneath can't be trusted. The bottleneck in turning data into an asset is not regulation but quality. That is why Pebblous diagnoses that quality through DataClinic.

## Facebook (EN)

I vaguely knew my information was being sold somewhere. I did not know for how much.

$319 billion. That is the size of the market the data brokers trade in.

The share that came back to me, the owner of that data, was $0.03.

When I first saw those numbers, what I felt was less anger than strangeness. "It's plainly mine, so why am I nowhere near the table where the price is set?"

Holding that question, I counted the posts I'd written over the past months and found them scattered in different places. Web3 reclaiming data sovereignty. The patent that puts a price tag on data. Korea's Digital Asset Basic Law, opening 12 business areas and a KRW 87.2 trillion market. The way ISO 5259 measures data quality with math. Looking back, each was saying the same sentence a different way. Data is no longer fuel; it is an asset.

An asset needs a price, an owner, and a grade. Yet however carefully you build those three, no one still vouches for whether the data itself can be trusted. The real key to opening the market turned out to be not the price tag but the quality beneath it.

That is why Pebblous has spent so long diagnosing data quality. Measuring the ground a price will lean on, before the price is set. The bigger the market grows, the more quietly and the earlier that work is needed.

Who decides what my data is worth? I'd be glad if you'd think it through with me.
