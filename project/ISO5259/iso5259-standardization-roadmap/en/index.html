Definition and Objectives of ISO/IEC 5259

ISO/IEC 5259 is an international standard specialized in data quality management for AI and machine learning. Its core objective is to institutionally manage the overarching principle of 'Garbage In, Garbage Out' -- that the performance of AI systems is determined by the quality of input data.

Role Differentiation with ISO/IEC 42001

üìã ISO/IEC 42001

AI Management System

Covers the overall 'management processes (operational procedures)' for organizations developing and operating AI systems. Focuses on management frameworks ensuring accountability, transparency, and fairness.

üìä ISO/IEC 5259

Data Quality

Deeply addresses the quality and lifecycle of the core 'material (data)' actually used within AI management systems. Defines specific quality characteristics such as accuracy, completeness, and consistency.

By analogy, if ISO/IEC 42001 sets the standards for 'safe recipes and kitchen operating rules', then ISO/IEC 5259 sets the standards for the 'fresh, clean ingredients' used in those recipes.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q. What is ISO/IEC 5259?

ISO/IEC 5259 is an international standard specialized in data quality management for AI and machine learning. Its goal is to institutionally manage the principle that AI system performance is determined by input data quality, providing criteria for systematically measuring and improving data quality.

Q. What is the difference between ISO/IEC 5259 and ISO/IEC 42001?

ISO/IEC 42001 covers the overall 'management processes (management system)' of AI systems, while ISO/IEC 5259 addresses the quality and lifecycle of the core 'material (data)' used in AI systems. By analogy, if 42001 is about 'recipes and kitchen operating rules', then 5259 is about 'standards for fresh ingredients'. The two standards are complementary.

Q. How does Pebblous Data Clinic relate to ISO 5259?

Pebblous's Data Clinic quantifies and implements the abstract quality characteristics (similarity, representativeness, diversity, efficiency) defined by ISO/IEC 5259 through patent-based technologies such as 'Data Imaging', 'Data Diet', and 'Data Bulk-up'. It serves as the only verified Missing Link that converts the standard's philosophical 'What' into an engineering 'How'.

Q. What is KOLAS accreditation and why is it important?

KOLAS is the Korea Laboratory Accreditation Scheme, a national system that officially recognizes that Pebblous's processes and technical competence for evaluating data quality meet international standards (ISO/IEC 17025). KOLAS accreditation serves as a bridgehead for entering domestic public procurement markets (Performance Certification, GS Certification) and as a 'passport for international trade' through ILAC MRA recognition overseas.

Q. Who received the world's first ISO/IEC 5259-3 certification?

SGS, the world's leading inspection and certification body, awarded the world's first ISO/IEC 5259-3 certification to construction technology company 'AI Clearing'. AI Clearing already held ISO/IEC 42001 (AI management system) certification, effectively building a complete AI governance framework managing both operational procedures and data at international standard levels.

Q. What competitive advantage do Pebblous's patent technologies provide?

Pebblous protects its technology with a robust patent portfolio including US Patent 12,481,720 B2 (registered November 2025, valid until 2042). This patent protects the Data Imaging and manifold learning methodology itself, serving as a barrier to entry when competitors attempt to implement similar diagnostic features to comply with ISO standards.
