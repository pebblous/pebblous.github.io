AI 스타트업을 인수할 때 실사표에 자산으로 오르던 학습 데이터가, 요즘은 부채로 다시 읽히기 시작했다.

국제 로펌 Mayer Brown은 7월 분석에서 합성 데이터가 이제 딜의 독자적 자산 클래스가 됐지만, 소유권·출처·품질이라는 세 가지 실사 리스크를 함께 떠안는다고 정리했다. 합성 데이터셋은 저작권이 성립하기 어렵고(Thaler v. Perlmutter), 원본의 침해 위험은 합성이라는 라벨 뒤에 그대로 상속되며(Bartz v. Anthropic), AI가 만든 데이터를 검증 없이 재사용하면 성능이 무너진다(Nature의 모델 붕괴 연구).

세 리스크의 공통점은 모두 장부 바깥에 있다는 것이다. 카탈로그에 적힌 테라바이트 숫자는 소유권이 성립하는지, 출처가 합법인지, 품질이 유지되는지를 한마디도 말해 주지 않는다.

검증된 데이터는 자산이고, 검증되지 않은 데이터는 대차대조표를 부풀린 부채다. 계보 추적·출처 검증·품질 관리라는 AI-Ready Data의 원칙이 규제와 성능을 넘어 기업가치를 지키는 실사 항목이 되는 순간이다.

## LinkedIn (EN)

The training data that used to sit on the asset side of an AI acquisition checklist is starting to read as a liability.

In a July analysis, the law firm Mayer Brown treats synthetic data as its own deal asset class, but one that carries three diligence risks at once: ownership, provenance, and quality. A synthetic dataset rarely qualifies for copyright (Thaler v. Perlmutter). The infringement risk of the source data is inherited straight through the synthetic label (Bartz v. Anthropic). And reusing AI-generated data without verification breaks model performance over generations (the Nature model-collapse study).

What ties the three together is that none of them show up on the books. The terabyte count in a data catalog says nothing about whether ownership holds, whether the source was lawful, or whether quality has held up.

Verified data is an asset. Unverified data is a liability that has inflated the balance sheet. Lineage tracing, provenance verification, and quality management — the AI-Ready Data principles — are becoming a diligence line item that protects enterprise value, not just a compliance or performance concern.

"Training data — tens of terabytes. Asset."

The number was large, and the label plainly read "asset." Yet that one line said nothing about whose data it really was, where it came from, or whether it still worked.

Reading Mayer Brown's analysis this month, that scene came back to me. Synthetic data is now priced as part of a deal's value, the firm writes, but without technical diligence on ownership, provenance, and quality, that asset stays on the acquirer's side as debt.

All three risks live off the books. A synthetic dataset rarely earns copyright. The infringement risk carried by the source data is quietly inherited behind the synthetic label. And AI-made data reused without verification lets a model decay generation after generation. None of the three appear in the catalog's numbers.

The lineage and quality of data that Pebblous has talked about for years returned this time in the language of valuation and contract clauses. What our diligence work with DataClinic really does is look at that line off the books.

So the first question stays with me.

The data we call an asset — would it still be an asset after diligence?
