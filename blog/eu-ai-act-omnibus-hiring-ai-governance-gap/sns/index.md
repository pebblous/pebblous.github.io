## LinkedIn (KO)

유럽의회와 이사회가 채용·신용평가 AI의 규제 시행을 2027년 12월로 16개월 미뤘다. 가장 민감한 AI에 가장 늦은 시계를 단 셈이다.

미뤄진 것은 의무의 내용이 아니라 시행일이다. 2026년 8월부터 약 18개월 동안, 채용 AI 벤더는 학습 데이터의 출처도 편향 검증 기록도 없이 EU 시장에 들어올 수 있다. 그사이 한 스탠퍼드 연구는 채용 알고리즘이 흑인 지원자의 4분의 1 이상을 능력과 무관하게 걸러낼 수 있다고 보고했다.

유예의 진짜 비용은 시간이 아니라 추적 가능성이다. 법이 잠시 눈을 감은 18개월 동안 내려진 결정은, 나중에 법이 눈을 떠도 거슬러 검증할 방법이 없다.

그래서 '법이 미뤄졌으니 거버넌스도 멈추자'는 판단이 이번 유예에서 가장 위험한 선택이 된다. 시행일은 미룰 수 있어도, 그동안 일어난 일의 기록은 나중에 만들어 낼 수 없다.

## LinkedIn (EN)

The European Parliament and Council have agreed to delay the EU AI Act's rules for hiring and credit-scoring AI by 16 months, to December 2027. The most sensitive class of AI was handed the latest clock.

What slipped is the deadline, not the duty. For roughly 18 months, vendors can enter the EU market without recording where their training data came from or whether it was tested for bias. During that window, the largest study of AI hiring algorithms to date found that more than a quarter of Black applicants could be screened out regardless of merit.

The real cost of the delay is not time but traceability. Decisions made while the law looks away cannot be audited once it looks back.

That makes "the law is delayed, so governance can wait" the riskiest read of this reprieve. A deadline can move; the record of what happened in between cannot be created after the fact.

## Facebook (EN)

The rejection email arrives a few days after you apply, and it never says why.

More and more, the line that decided it wasn't written by a person. It was scored by a model whose training data, and whose reasons for filtering you out, the company itself often can't fully explain.

In June, the European Parliament and Council agreed to delay the rules for exactly this kind of hiring and credit-scoring AI until December 2027. The most sensitive class of AI was handed the latest clock.

What moved was the deadline, not the duty. For about 18 months, a model with no record of where its data came from or whether it was tested for bias can lawfully enter the market and screen résumés. One study found that in such systems, more than a quarter of Black applicants could be filtered out regardless of merit.

I keep coming back to the thought that the real cost of this delay isn't time. It's the record. A decision made while the law looks away can't be reconstructed once it looks back, because a record can only be kept while the thing is still happening.

That is what makes "the law is delayed, so we can wait too" the riskiest choice of all. It is also why Pebblous works on tracing where training data comes from and how it was checked. A deadline can be postponed. The reason someone was turned away cannot.
