More than 220 former unicorns have fallen below a $1 billion valuation, with companies that last raised in 2021 losing an average of 68% of their value.

SaaS and marketing tech absorbed the heaviest losses. Why now? AI now performs directly what those products built subscription models around. AI-native companies, meanwhile, have commanded revenue multiples of ten times or more — the opposite trajectory.

Down rounds trigger investor resistance; strategic acquirers are increasingly building in-house or buying AI-native firms outright. The exit door is narrower for everyone else.

Whether the path is rebuilding AI-native or fortifying a narrow domain, clean and structured data is the prerequisite. That is why Pebblous focuses on AI-Ready data.

The investor question has compressed to one: AI-native or not?

## Facebook (EN)

"Is this company AI-native or not?"

That single question has replaced the due-diligence checklist in investor meetings. When I read that, what stayed with me was not the efficiency of the question but what it means for founders on the other side of it.

Startups that last raised in 2021 have lost an average of 68% of their value. More than 220 former unicorns have fallen below the billion-dollar threshold.

The reason is not arbitrary. AI now performs directly what SaaS companies built subscription models around. The revenue justification broke. Meanwhile, AI-native companies collected the opposite result, with multiples well above what traditional software ever commanded.

"If down rounds, IPOs, and acquisitions are all blocked — what is actually possible?"

That question sits at the center of this piece. Pebblous focuses on data quality and AI-Ready data infrastructure, and for founders on either path (rebuilding AI-native or fortifying a narrow domain), clean and structured data is where that answer begins.

What roads remain for companies built before ChatGPT. We tried to follow the data.
