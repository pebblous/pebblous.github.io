## LinkedIn (EN)

AgiBot has started deliberately collecting the moments robots drop, bump, and let things slip.

Released on June 3, the second theme of AGIBOT WORLD 2026 — "Rich Interaction" — moves in the opposite direction from datasets built on clean, successful demonstrations. A bimanual humanoid, G2, synchronizes camera video with the gripper's tactile and force signals on a single timeline, turning the texture of contact itself into something recorded rather than discarded.

A model trained only on successful clips copies the look of an action but never learns the physics beneath it. When a hand begins to slip, and how much more force stops it, happens at the fingertips, not inside the camera frame.

The next bottleneck in robot data is not volume but what gets recorded, and at what fidelity. The modality you leave out sets the ceiling of the data.

그런데 아지봇은 며칠 전, 정반대의 데이터를 공개했습니다.

로봇이 물건을 놓치고, 부딪히고, 미끄러뜨리고, 액체를 흘리는 순간을 일부러 모은 데이터입니다. 그리고 그 접촉의 질감을, 눈에 보이는 영상만이 아니라 손끝의 촉각 신호로 함께 기록했습니다.

저는 이 선택 앞에서 한 가지 질문에 오래 머물렀습니다.

"완벽한 예시만 보여 주면 모델도 완벽을 배울까?"

사람도 눈을 감고 손끝 감각만으로 컵을 들 수 있지만, 손끝을 마취하면 같은 동작이 위태로워집니다. 손이 언제 미끄러지기 시작하는지, 힘을 얼마나 더 줘야 멈추는지는 픽셀이 아니라 손끝에서 일어나는 일이기 때문입니다. 성공 영상만 모은 데이터에는 그 손끝의 감각이 비어 있습니다.

페블러스가 데이터 품질을 '얼마나 많이 모았는가'가 아니라 '무엇을 어떤 충실도로 남겼는가'의 문제로 보는 이유도 여기에 있습니다. 접촉의 질감을 데이터로 남기는 일은, 이제 막 시작된 것 같습니다.

## Facebook (EN)

A robot picking up a cup cleanly, setting it down cleanly.

For years, robot datasets collected only these tidy successes, and treated the slips and bumps in between as noise to be erased.

A few days ago, AgiBot released the opposite.

It is data made of the moments a robot drops things, collides, lets them slip, spills liquid. And the texture of that contact was recorded not only in what the camera could see, but in the tactile signals at the fingertips.

I kept returning to one question.

"If we only show a model perfect examples, does it actually learn the physics?"

You can lift a cup with your eyes closed, using only the feeling in your fingertips. Numb those fingertips, and the same motion turns precarious. When a hand begins to slip, and how much more force will stop it, happens at the fingertips, not in the pixels. Data made only of successes leaves that fingertip sense empty.

The bottleneck in robot data is usually framed as volume. But even within the same hour of recording, what you capture and at what fidelity is what sets the ceiling.

This is also why Pebblous treats data quality less as a question of how much was gathered, and more as a question of what was kept, and how faithfully. Leaving the texture of contact behind as data feels like something that has only just begun.
