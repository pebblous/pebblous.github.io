## LinkedIn (KO)

AI 에이전트가 실제 회사 업무에 붙는 순간, 열에 아홉은 무너진다.

벤스포크랩스가 바로 그 실패를 정면으로 겨눈 강화학습 환경으로 시리즈A 4천만 달러를 받았다. 이들이 파는 것은 모델이 아니라 무대다. 대형 코드베이스와 서로 호출하는 마이크로서비스, 사실적인 서버 로그, 쌓여 있는 지원 티켓, 오가는 이메일과 슬랙까지 갖춘 '가짜 회사'를 통째로 짓는다. 에이전트가 그 안에서 마음껏 실수하고 되돌리며 며칠짜리 업무를 연습하는 곳이다.

돈이 이쪽으로 흐르는 이유는 진단에 있다. 에이전트 프로젝트의 70~95%가 프로덕션에 닿지 못하는데, 원인으로 지목된 건 모델의 지능이 아니라 기저 데이터 인프라의 취약함과 긴 작업의 오류 관리였다. 병목이 얼마나 똑똑한 모델인가에서 무엇으로 훈련시켰는가로 옮겨가고 있다.

다만 진짜 난제는 데이터를 모으는 일이 아니라 검증하는 일이다. 며칠짜리 업무에는 정답이 하나로 떨어지지 않는다. 이미 서른다섯 곳 넘는 업체가 같은 자리를 노리고 뛰어들었고, 재현과 검증을 산업화한 소수만 살아남을 것이다.

신뢰할 수 있는 에이전트는 더 똑똑한 모델이 아니라, 진본에 가깝게 재현된 환경에서 나온다. 환경은 은유가 아니라 데이터의 한 형태다.

## LinkedIn (EN)

Put an AI agent inside a real company's workflow, and it breaks far more often than the demo suggests.

Bespoke Labs just raised a $40M Series A aimed squarely at that failure. What it sells is not a model but a stage: a full "fake company" of large codebases, interlocking microservices, realistic server logs, support tickets, email, and Slack. Inside it, agents are free to mess up, roll back, and retry their way through multi-day work.

The money follows a diagnosis. Between 70% and 95% of agent projects never reach production, and the culprit is not model intelligence but fragile data infrastructure and poor error handling over long tasks. The bottleneck is shifting from how smart the model is to what it was trained on.

The harder problem, though, is not collecting data but verifying it. Multi-day tasks rarely have a single correct answer. More than 35 startups are already chasing this market, and only the few that industrialize reproduction and verification will survive.

Reliable agents come not from a smarter model but from an environment reproduced close to the real thing. The environment is not a metaphor. It is a form of data.

## Facebook (EN)

An agent that moves flawlessly in the demo, then touches the wrong file and stalls the moment it enters a real company's workflow.

If you have seen that scene, you can probably guess where Bespoke Labs' $40M is headed.

What they build is not a model but a stage.

A "fake company" where agents are free to fail: codebases, microservices, a backlog of tickets, threads of Slack messages — a world that looks and behaves like a real one. Inside it, agents mess up, roll back, and slowly learn work that spans days.

The money follows a quiet diagnosis. Most agent projects never reach production, and what holds them back is not the model's intelligence but the data infrastructure underneath it. So the question shifts. Not how smart the model is, but what it lived through while learning.

The truly hard part, they say, is not collecting the data but verifying it. Multi-day work rarely has a single right answer. There are many paths to the same result, and someone still has to decide how to grade that ambiguity.

This is where AI-Ready Data, something Pebblous has talked about for a long time, comes back into view. Where we once pictured tidy static tables, we now find the trajectory of an agent acting, failing, and reversing course — the experience itself becoming the material to learn from.

If reliable agents come not from a smarter model but from an environment reproduced close to the real thing, how far should the meaning of "preparing data" stretch?
