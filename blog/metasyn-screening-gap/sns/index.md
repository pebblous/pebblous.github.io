칭화대가 공개한 MetaSyn 벤치마크는 전문가 메타분석 442편을 정답지로 두고, 검색 단계와 선별 단계를 따로 채점했다. 그러자 한 점수에 가려졌던 병목이 드러났다. 정답이 어디 있는지는 거의 다 찾아내는데, 찾아온 후보 중 무엇이 기준에 맞는지를 가려내는 데서 38%p가 빠졌다.

원인은 쿼리 하나의 구성에 압축돼 있다. 포함 대상 약 16편 옆에, 주제는 닮았지만 PI/ECO 기준이 어긋난 유사 논문이 약 184편 깔려 있었다. 함정이 진짜보다 열한 배 많았던 셈이다. 더 역설적인 건 검색을 정교하게 만들수록 풀이 닮은 것들로 빽빽해져 선별이 더 어려워졌다는 점이다.

AI의 한계는 정보를 못 찾는 데 있지 않았다. 무엇이 기준에 맞는 데이터인지를 판정하는 데 있었다. 검색이 끝나는 자리에서 데이터 품질이 시작된다.

## LinkedIn (EN)

AI pulled 90.9% of the right papers out of a 140,000-paper pool. Then it picked only 52.7% of the ones that actually belonged in the meta-analysis.

MetaSyn, a benchmark from Tsinghua University, grades retrieval and screening separately against 442 expert meta-analyses from Nature Portfolio. Splitting the two stages exposes a bottleneck a single score would hide: AI already knows where the answers are, but loses 38 points when it has to decide which retrieved candidates actually meet the criteria.

The reason sits inside a single query. About 16 papers belong, while roughly 184 lookalikes sit beside them, similar in topic but off on the PI/ECO criteria. Stranger still, a better retriever made screening worse, because a sharper net packs the pool with even denser near-misses.

The limit was never finding information. It was judging which data fits the standard. Where retrieval ends, data quality begins.

## Facebook (EN)

Finding and choosing are not the same thing.

Anyone who has gathered a large pile of material knows the hard part was never the gathering. It was sorting out which pieces actually belonged.

A benchmark released this June handed exactly that task to an AI: out of 140,000 papers, find the ones that belong in a meta-analysis, and pick them.

The AI found 90.9% of the right papers. The searching was nearly done.

But it chose only 52.7% of them. It hadn't failed to find the rest; it failed to filter out the papers that looked similar yet missed the criteria. Beside the 16 that belonged, some 184 plausible lookalikes were waiting in every query.

The stranger part: the better the search got, the harder the choosing became. A finer net only packed the pool with denser near-misses.

"Does this paper meet the standard?"

That question has the same shape as "what counts as good data?" Collecting keeps getting easier, and the hard work moves toward judgment.

Maybe data quality begins right where the search ends.
