## LinkedIn (EN)

Meta wasn't taken to court because Llama trained on books. It was taken to court over how it got them: 267 terabytes pulled from shadow libraries via torrent, with IP addresses masked and copyright notices stripped, according to the complaint.

The pattern is already settled law. Anthropic won on 'training is fair use' and still lost on 'downloading and keeping pirated copies,' settling for $1.5 billion — the largest publicly reported recovery in U.S. copyright history. Once courts separate training from acquisition, 'unclear provenance' stops being a governance note and becomes an invoice priced per title.

So the first document a model buyer asks for is changing. Not a benchmark score, but the data's lineage. The EU AI Act begins mandating training-data disclosure in August, and in procurement, 78% of AI data partners cannot verify their own training data.

The next due-diligence file may not be the weights. It may be the data's family tree. That is why Pebblous uses DataClinic to quantitatively diagnose the quality and provenance of AI training data.

## Facebook (EN)

Imagine buying an AI model.

You've seen the benchmark scores. You've run the demo. Then someone asks the one question that appears on no scorecard: where did this model's training data come from?

That question is exactly what played out in court this year.

Meta wasn't sued because Llama learned from books. It was sued over how it got them: torrented from shadow libraries, with the traces quietly scrubbed. What stood trial wasn't the model. It was the doorway the data came through.

Anthropic reached that same doorway a step earlier. It won on fair use for the training itself, and still paid $1.5 billion for downloading and keeping pirated copies. A single line, 'provenance unclear,' was translated into an invoice with a price tag on every single book.

I keep returning to what this quietly signals to anyone who works with data. The worth of a model is sliding away from performance and toward something older: the ability to explain where it came from.

Where did this data come from? Can you answer in one sentence?
