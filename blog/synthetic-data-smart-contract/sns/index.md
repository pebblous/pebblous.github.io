### EN

The synthetic data market is on track to reach $2.63B by 2030.

Yet a fundamental question remains unanswered:

**How do you trust and trade synthetic data?**

There's no standardized way to verify quality before purchase. Provenance can't be traced. Usage terms are still managed through manual contracts. The synthetic data market is trapped in a classic lemon market structure — and nobody has built the exchange yet.

Pebblous Patent No. 10-2969395 (registered May 2026) addresses this head-on.

The core idea comes down to three things:
**Virtual environment × Quality proof × Automated trade.**

- Validate synthetic data in a simulation of real operating conditions before any deal
- Wire validation results directly into smart contract execution logic
- Record every step — generation to governance — on an immutable blockchain

It's the technical backbone of the Data Greenhouse pipeline: DataClinic (diagnose) → PebbloSim (generate) → PebbloChain (trade & govern).

We explore how this patent fills the missing infrastructure layer in the synthetic data economy.

### EN

When I first heard the term "synthetic data," I was skeptical.

"If it's not real data, who's going to trust it?"

But the market moved differently. NVIDIA acquired Gretel for over $320M in March 2025. SAS acquired Hazy. Gartner projects 75% of enterprises will be using synthetic data by 2026.

The market is hot. So why are transactions still cold?

The answer is a missing trust infrastructure. No way to verify quality before purchase. No system to trace provenance. No automated contract to govern how data gets used — it all still runs on handshakes and PDFs.

The Pebblous patent filed three years ago and registered this May is a blueprint for filling that gap. Three layers: virtual environment for pre-trade validation, smart contracts for automated execution, blockchain for immutable provenance records.

A patent isn't an answer. It's a starting point. But drawing the starting point is harder than it looks.
