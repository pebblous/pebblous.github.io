## LinkedIn (KO)

EU가 자체 프론티어 AI를 짓겠다고 발표했다. 정작 눈길을 끈 숫자는 모델 크기가 아니었다.

2026년 6월 19일, 집행위는 이탈리아 Domyn이 주도하는 EUROPA 컨소시엄을 선정했다. 4천억 매개변수 오픈소스 모델을 EU 24개 공식 언어 전부로, 1년 안에 내놓겠다는 조건이다. 컴퓨팅은 EuroHPC 슈퍼컴퓨터 용량의 2.5%를 1년치 떼어 줬다.

24개 언어를 못 박는 순간, 진짜 시험대는 모델의 크기가 아니라 가장 작은 언어의 데이터가 된다. 몰타어가 거대 웹 말뭉치 Common Crawl에서 차지하는 비중은 0.03%에 불과하다.

가장 큰 유럽 오픈소스 모델로 꼽히는 EuroLLM도 학습 데이터의 절반이 영어이고, 비주력 EU 언어 전체를 합쳐도 9%다. 모델을 키운다고 없던 몰타어 문장이 생기지는 않는다. 그 데이터를 누가 어떤 품질로 모을지는 아직 합의되지 않았다.

주권 AI의 병목은 컴퓨트가 아니라 준비된 자국어 데이터다.

## LinkedIn (EN)

The EU announced it would build its own frontier AI. The number that mattered wasn't the model's size.

On June 19, 2026, the Commission selected the EUROPA consortium, led by the Italian firm Domyn, as winner of its Frontier AI Grand Challenge. The mandate: a 400-billion-parameter open-source model trained on all 24 official EU languages, delivered within a year. Compute comes from 2.5% of the EuroHPC supercomputers for twelve months.

The moment you make 24 languages a condition, the real test shifts from model scale to the smallest language's data. Maltese accounts for just 0.03% of Common Crawl, the standard web corpus.

EuroLLM, the largest European open-source model to date, still draws half its training data from English, with every non-major EU language combined reaching only 9%. A bigger model does not conjure Maltese sentences that were never written. Who will collect that data, and to what quality standard, remains unsettled.

The bottleneck for sovereign AI is not compute. It is ready, native-language data.

## Facebook (EN)

I keep thinking about someone asking an AI a question in Maltese.

The same model that answers fluently in English often stumbles when the question arrives in a smaller language.

When the EU announced on June 19 that it would build its own frontier AI, the headline was the model's size: 400 billion parameters. But the number the announcement mentioned most briefly is the one that stayed with me.

Maltese makes up 0.03% of the giant web corpus that trains these models.

The promise to carry all 24 official languages is the right instinct. Yet the moment language becomes a condition, the real test moves from the model's scale to the smallest language's data. The compute was carved out of a supercomputer. But where do the Maltese and Latvian sentences come from, and who guarantees they are clean enough to learn from?

The day a model's weights go public and the day a citizen of that language holds an equal AI may not be the same day.

Sovereignty doesn't arrive from training one large model once. It arrives when a community can hold the flow of its own language data in its own hands — gathering it, cleaning it, checking it, over and over. That is why Pebblous reads this announcement through the state of the data rather than the size of the model.
