## LinkedIn (KO)

데이터브릭스의 기업가치가 5개월 만에 40% 올라 1,880억 달러가 됐다.

정작 눈여겨볼 것은 그 숫자가 아니라 새 자금이 향하는 곳이다. 회사가 밝힌 용처는 더 큰 모델이 아니라, 어떤 AI가 어떤 업무를 맡을지 제어하고 비용과 접근을 감시하는 Unity AI Gateway 같은 거버넌스 제품군이다. CEO 알리 고드시는 이 전환을 "토큰 최대화(tokenmaxxing)에서 가치 최대화(valuemaxxing)로"라고 불렀다. 가장 비싼 모델의 토큰을 아무 데나 태우는 대신 달러당 최선의 결과를 얻겠다는 것이다.

GPU를 빌려주는 투게더 AI, 데이터에 권한과 감사를 심은 페레그린에 이어 세 번째다. 모델이 흔해질수록 값은 그 모델을 무엇으로 먹이고 어떻게 통제하느냐로 옮겨간다.

다만 그 거버넌스를 기술 문서까지 열어 보면, 지금 손에 쥔 것은 접근 로그와 데이터 계보 그래프다. "이 데이터가 더 나은 결과를 냈다"를 증명하는 지표는 아직 비어 있다.

다스리는 층에 값이 붙는 흐름은 분명하다. 남은 질문은 그 다스림이 실제로 결과를 냈는지 검증하는 다음 층이다.

## LinkedIn (EN)

Databricks is raising at a $188 billion valuation, up 40% in just five months.

The number isn't the story. The new capital isn't funding a bigger model. The company's stated use is a set of governance products led by Unity AI Gateway, which decides which AI handles which task and watches what it costs and who can touch it. CEO Ali Ghodsi calls the shift "tokenmaxxing to valuemaxxing": instead of burning the priciest model's tokens on everything, wanting the best result per dollar.

It is the third such round after Together AI's GPU infrastructure and Peregrine's governance data layer. As models commoditize, value moves to how you feed and control them.

Yet open that governance to its technical docs and what you hold today is access logs and a data lineage graph, not a metric proving the data produced a better outcome.

The direction is clear: capital is pricing the layer that governs data. The open question is the next layer, the one that verifies the governing actually worked.

## Facebook (EN)

A single number drifted through my feed this week. Databricks had gone from a $134 billion valuation to $188 billion in five months.

At first it read as just another oversized round.

What made me stop wasn't the figure. It was where the money is going. Not toward a bigger model. The company says the capital will fund governance products, the layer that decides which AI handles which task and watches what it costs and who can touch it.

Their CEO gave the shift a name: "tokenmaxxing to valuemaxxing." Instead of burning the most expensive model's tokens on everything, wanting the best result per dollar.

The scene felt familiar. Together AI, renting out GPUs. Peregrine, binding permission and audit into the data itself. Each time, capital priced the same place. The layer that governs data, not the model.

One thing kept nagging, though.

"How far does governing actually reach?"

Open that governance to its technical docs and what you hold today is a record of who touched what and when, and a graph of where the data flowed. Valuable records. But whether they led to a better outcome, or prevented harm, is still unmeasured.

The logs accumulate. What they protected, no one yet counts.

This is why Pebblous talks about AI-Ready Data as quality and governance held together. After you make data safe to use, the work that remains is confirming it actually stayed that way. The market has started pricing the layer that governs. The next question is probably what proves the governing worked.
