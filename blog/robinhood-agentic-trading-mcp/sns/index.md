## LinkedIn (EN)

On May 27, Robinhood let AI agents trade stocks and swipe credit cards on behalf of its 27.5 million customers.

Tell ChatGPT or Claude to "lower my volatility," and the agent decides when to buy and sell, then places the order itself. AI has moved from advising to pulling the trigger.

The plumbing is MCP. For nearly two years the protocol let code editors read repositories; now it moves money inside real brokerage accounts.

But the terms are blunt. Every trade an agent makes is the user's responsibility, and Robinhood says it does not supervise or audit the agents. Five months earlier, FINRA had already drawn the line: when AI acts rather than just generates, a firm's oversight duties shift.

Autonomy is delegated; liability is not. The only thing that can bridge the gap is data that is traceable and verified.

## Facebook (EN)

"Make my portfolio more profitable."

Say that sentence to an AI now, not to a person, and it will pick the stocks, decide when to buy and sell, and place the order itself.

On May 27, Robinhood opened this to 27.5 million customers. For years AI sat in the advisor's seat, telling us what we might do. Now it pulls the trigger.

The backbone is a protocol called MCP. For nearly two years it let code editors read repositories. Now it moves money in real brokerage accounts. Same standard, but what it touches has shifted from text to assets.

What stayed with me was a single line in the terms. Every trade the agent makes is the user's responsibility, and Robinhood neither supervises nor audits it. Autonomy is fully delegated; so is the liability.

The regulators saw it coming. Five months before launch, FINRA had already written that once AI begins to act, a firm's oversight duties change.

So I keep returning to one question.

"If an agent is going to move money on our behalf, what shape must the data beneath its judgment be in?"

The more autonomous the agent, the more the center of trust shifts from a smarter model to the data underneath it. That is why Pebblous has spent so long making data AI-Ready. Traceable, verified data — perhaps that is the agent's last guardrail.
