## LinkedIn (EN)

Companies bought smarter models. Seven of every eight pilots built on them never reach production. That is IDC's 88%.

Upgrading the model barely moves the number. MIT found that 80% of the work of taking a pilot to production is data engineering, governance, and integration. Gartner traced 85% of failed AI projects to data quality. The model was fine. The data was not ready.

Of the roughly $684B spent on AI in 2025, about $547B produced no measurable return. Yet the few pilots that did reach production averaged a 171% ROI. The same model returns nothing for most and value for a few. The fork was never the model; it was the path the data takes to reach it.

So the question shifts from "which model should we buy" to "is our data ready for production." After an expensive lesson, the market confirmed an old premise: data comes before the model.

페블러스가 데이터의 신선도와 계보, 품질을 줄곧 살펴 온 이유도 거기에 있습니다. 모델을 얹기 전에, 그 모델이 닿을 데이터가 production에서 살아 있는지부터 확인하는 일입니다. 모델을 한 단계 올리는 일보다, 데이터가 새어 나가는 길목을 먼저 막는 일. 비싼 수업료를 치른 시장이 결국 도착한 자리가 거기였습니다.

## Facebook (EN)

"The demo works. So why can't we ship it?"

More AI projects are stuck on that exact sentence than anyone likes to admit.

Seven out of every eight pilots quietly disappear there. That is what IDC's 88% looks like up close.

The industry has a name for the state: pilot purgatory. Too good to kill, too fragile to run. The budget keeps flowing; the value never arrives.

What kept nagging me was the timing. Models get better every year, yet the share of pilots reaching production refuses to climb with them. If a stronger model doesn't move the number, the blockage isn't inside the model. It sits just outside it.

MIT and Gartner pointed to the same place. Most of the work of moving a pilot into production turns out to be preparing, connecting, and governing data. The model was fine. The data was not ready.

So the question I keep returning to is quieter than "which model is best." It is "can we get our data ready before we buy the next one?"

That is also where Pebblous keeps looking: at the freshness, lineage, and quality of data, before any model is placed on top of it. Closing the path where data leaks out, rather than upgrading the model one more notch. After an expensive lesson, that is where the market arrived.
