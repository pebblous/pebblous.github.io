## LinkedIn (EN)

SAP just declared the autonomous enterprise: more than 200 specialist agents running finance, supply chain, procurement, and HR. One of them, the Autonomous Close agent, already touches the general ledger and compresses a weeks-long financial close into days.

There's one thing these actors are missing: identity. When a person is hired, an employee ID, permissions, and logs follow automatically. Agents get none of that. In a survey of 383 security professionals, 78% of organizations had no policy for creating or revoking agent identities, and 68% couldn't tell agent actions apart from human ones.

So the real infrastructure of the autonomous enterprise isn't a smarter model. It's the data identity and lineage that records who touched which data and what they changed. Identity answers for the actor; lineage answers for the data.

Autonomy can't outrun the maturity of your data governance.

행위자는 분명히 있는데, 그 행위자의 정체가 기록에 남지 않은 겁니다.

올해 5월 SAP는 200개가 넘는 전문 에이전트가 업무를 끝까지 실행하는 '자율 기업'을 선언했습니다. 그중 하나는 회계 장부를 직접 만지며 몇 주 걸리던 재무 마감을 며칠로 줄입니다.

그런데 회사를 돌리는 이 새로운 행위자들에게는 그 뼈대가 빠져 있습니다. 어느 조사에서는 열에 여덟에 가까운 조직이 에이전트의 신원을 만들고 없애는 정책조차 갖고 있지 않았습니다.

저는 이 질문이 오래 남았습니다. "당신 회사의 에이전트는 직원입니까, 유령입니까?"

직원이라면 누가 무엇을 했는지 되짚을 수 있어야 하고, 그러려면 데이터가 자기 출처를 기억하고 있어야 합니다. 페블러스가 AI-Ready Data를 이야기할 때 들여다보는 자리도 결국 여기입니다.

자율화의 속도가 아무리 빨라도, 그 속도는 데이터가 자기 내력을 기억하는 깊이를 넘지 못하는 것 같습니다.

## Facebook (EN)

An auditor opens the log and can't tell which change came from an employee's hand and which came from an agent running on its own.

The actor was clearly there. Its identity simply wasn't.

In May, SAP declared the autonomous enterprise: more than 200 specialist agents carrying work all the way to completion. One of them touches the general ledger directly, turning a financial close that used to take weeks into a matter of days.

When a person joins a company, an employee ID and permissions and logs are laid down first, so that later we can reconstruct who did what. That scaffolding comes before the work.

The new actors running the business don't have it. In one survey, close to eight in ten organizations had no policy even for creating and retiring an agent's identity.

The question that stayed with me was a simple one. "Is the agent in your company an employee, or a ghost?"

If it's an employee, you should be able to retrace what it did, and for that the data itself has to remember where it came from. That is the place Pebblous keeps pointing to when it talks about AI-Ready Data.

However fast autonomy moves, it doesn't seem to outrun the depth at which data remembers its own history.
