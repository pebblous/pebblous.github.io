## LinkedIn (KO)

Llama를 가중치째 세상에 풀던 메타가, 새 모델 Muse Spark는 API 뒤에 잠갔다.

지난 4월에 공개된 이 모델은 가중치도 아키텍처도 내놓지 않는다. 메타가 문을 닫으며 든 명분은 둘이다. DeepSeek가 Llama 구조를 베이스로 자기 모델을 증류해 갔다는 '복제', 그리고 한번 푼 가중치는 되돌릴 수 없다는 '안전'이다.

결이 다른 두 이야기지만 한 겹만 벗기면 같은 질문에 닿는다. 이 모델은 무엇을 학습했고, 그 안에 어떤 능력과 위험이 들어 있는가. 모델을 닫으면 그 답인 학습 데이터의 투명성도 함께 닫힌다.

그런데 닫는다고 끝나지 않는다. 8월 2일, EU AI법은 모든 범용 AI의 학습 데이터 공개 의무에 완전 집행 권한을 발동한다. 가중치를 잠가도 요약 공개 의무는 그대로 남고, 오히려 오픈소스 면제를 잃어 더 무거운 부담을 질 수도 있다.

데이터가 무엇으로 이뤄졌는지를 진단해 온 회사로서 페블러스가 이 전환을 주목하는 이유다. 모델의 해자가 공개성에서 데이터 통제로 옮겨가도, 답해야 할 질문은 그대로다. 당신의 모델은 무엇으로 만들어졌는가.

## LinkedIn (EN)

Meta opened Llama to the world weight by weight. Its new model, Muse Spark, it locked behind an API.

Released in April, the model ships with neither weights nor architecture disclosed. Meta gives two reasons for closing the door. One is cloning: DeepSeek distilled its own models on top of Llama's architecture. The other is safety: once weights are public, they can never be pulled back.

The two arguments sound different, but peel back a layer and they meet at the same question. What did this model learn, and what capability and risk live inside it? Close the model, and the answer to that question, the transparency of its training data, closes with it.

Closing it does not settle the matter. On August 2, the EU AI Act gains full enforcement power over training-data disclosure for every general-purpose model. Locked weights do not lift the summary obligation, and by forfeiting the open-source exemption, a closed model may carry a heavier burden than an open one.

That is why Pebblous, a company that diagnoses what data is made of, keeps watching this pivot. The moat may move from openness to data control, but the question stays the same. What was your model made of?

## Facebook (EN)

It looked like one hand holding a door open while the other quietly turned a lock.

For years, Meta was the company that put Llama into the world weight by weight. On top of that openness, the pipelines of countless labs and startups grew.

This April, that same company locked its first closed model, Muse Spark, behind an API.

The reasons it gave were two. A rival had built on Llama's structure, the "cloning" worry. And weights, once released, can never be recalled, the "safety" worry.

They sound like separate stories. Sit with them, and both point to the same place.

"What did this model learn, and what risk lives inside it?"

To close a model is also to move the answer to that question, what its training data actually was, out of public view.

Yet closing it does not make the question go away. In August, the EU AI Act begins asking even closed models to explain their training data. To control what a model learned is to take on the duty of explaining what it learned.

That is the reason Pebblous, a company that diagnoses what data is made of, keeps returning to this pivot.

Open or closed, one question seems to remain.

What was your model made of?
