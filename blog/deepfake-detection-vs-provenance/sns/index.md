2026년 유타밸리대학교 실험 결과다. 참가자들은 오히려 딥페이크에 진짜 영상과 같거나 더 높은 신뢰 점수를 매겼다. 위협은 "정교한 가짜일수록 위험하다"가 아니라 "진짜와 구분되지 않는다"는 데 있었다.

방어선을 맡아 온 탐지 기술은 광고된 90%대 정확도가 실전에서 50%대로 주저앉는다. 탐지기는 자기가 학습한 생성 방식만 잘 잡아내고, 새 모델이 나올 때마다 다시 학습하기 전까지 뒤처지기 때문이다. 만드는 쪽이 언제나 한 발 앞선다.

그래서 질문을 바꿔야 한다. "이 영상이 가짜인가"를 쫓는 대신 "이 영상이 어디서 왔는가"를 증명하는 쪽으로. C2PA와 Content Credentials는 콘텐츠가 만들어지는 시점에 검증 가능한 출처를 새겨 둔다.

다만 만능은 아니다. 주요 SNS를 한 번 거치거나 화면을 한 번 캡처하면 출처 메타데이터는 사실상 전부 사라진다. 정작 콘텐츠가 가장 빠르게 퍼지는 길목에서 출처가 가장 먼저 떨어져 나간다.

콘텐츠의 진본성 문제는 결국 데이터의 진본성 문제와 같은 자리에서 만난다. 페블러스가 출처추적과 데이터 진본성을 데이터 품질의 한 축으로 다루는 이유다.

## LinkedIn (EN)

When 632 people watched the same message — half as authentic video, half as a deepfake — the shift in their voting intention showed no statistically meaningful difference.

That is the finding of a 2026 Utah Valley University experiment. If anything, participants rated the deepfakes as equally or more credible than the real footage. The danger was never that synthetic media looks too polished. It is that it has become indistinguishable from the real thing.

Detection, long treated as the front line, does not hold up outside the lab. Vendor-advertised accuracy of 90 to 99 percent collapses to the 50 to 65 percent range on content the model has not been trained on. A detector only recognizes the generation methods it has already studied, so every new model puts it a step behind. The forger always moves first.

The question has to flip. Instead of endlessly asking "is this fake?", we move the standard of trust to "where did this come from?" C2PA and Content Credentials sign a verifiable history into content at the moment it is created.

But provenance is no silver bullet. A single pass through a major social platform, or a single screenshot, strips that metadata almost entirely. The trail disappears exactly where content travels fastest.

Content authenticity and data authenticity turn out to be the same question. That is why Pebblous treats provenance and data lineage as a core axis of data quality.

우리는 오랫동안 "정교한 가짜일수록 위험하다"고 믿어 왔습니다. 그런데 이 실험이 가리키는 자리는 조금 다릅니다. 위협은 가짜가 화려해서가 아니라, 진짜와 구분되지 않아서 생긴다는 것이었습니다.

진짜를 증명할 수 있는 능력. 다음 시대의 신뢰는 아마 그 위에 세워질 것 같습니다.

## Facebook (EN)

"Is this one real?"

I catch myself pausing on that question more and more while scrolling.

This year, researchers at Utah Valley University ran a quiet experiment with 632 people. Half watched a real person on video. Half watched a deepfake carrying the very same message.

The shift in how they intended to vote was the same on both sides.

If anything, the deepfakes earned slightly higher trust.

For a long time we assumed the more polished the fake, the more dangerous it must be. This study points somewhere else. The threat is not that synthetic video has become dazzling. It is that it has become indistinguishable from the real thing.

So why not lean on detection to catch the fakes? Outside the lab, the advertised ninety-some-percent accuracy slips down to barely better than a coin flip. Each time a new generation model appears, the catchers can only respond after they have studied it. They are always a step late.

That is when a different question started to feel more honest. Instead of forever asking "is this fake?", what if we asked "where did this come from?"

Provenance standards like C2PA sign a verifiable history into a piece of content at the moment it is made. And yet there is a hole in this, too. A single trip through the social platforms where we share the most strips almost all of that origin away.

In the end, asking where a video came from and asking where a dataset came from turn out to be the same question. Where is this from, who changed it, when, and how? That is why Pebblous looks at data authenticity and lineage as part of quality itself.

The ability to prove what is real. The trust of the next era may well be built on top of it.
