## LinkedIn (KO)

8월 2일부터 EU에서 챗봇은 첫 마디에 자신이 사람이 아니라 AI임을 밝혀야 한다.

지난 5월 AI Omnibus 합의로 "규제가 미뤄졌다"는 소식에 안심한 기업이 많았다. 정확히는 채용·신용평가에 쓰이는 고위험 AI 의무만 2027년 이후로 연장됐고, 챗봇 공개와 딥페이크 라벨링 같은 투명성 의무는 8월 2일 그대로 켜진다.

적용 기준은 회사가 어디에 있느냐가 아니다. GDPR과 같은 구조로, AI 출력물이 EU 안에서 쓰이면 EU 법인이 없는 한국·미국 기업에도 그대로 닿는다. 그보다 앞선 7월 22일에는 Code of Practice 서명 마감이 있다. 서명한 기업은 준수 추정을 받아, 문제가 생겼을 때 입증 책임이 기업이 아니라 당국으로 넘어간다.

투명성 의무의 바닥에는 결국 "이 출력이 어디서 왔는가"를 추적하고 문서화하는 데이터 거버넌스가 있다. 페블러스가 DataClinic으로 다뤄 온 데이터 출처·품질 검증이 마침 그 자리에 있다. 적용 대상의 78%가 아직 손도 대지 않은 지금, 남은 6주는 비용이 아니라 EU 조달 경쟁에서 앞설 시간이다.

## LinkedIn (EN)

From August 2, any chatbot serving EU users must disclose, up front, that it is not human.

Many companies relaxed in May when the AI Omnibus deal was read as "the rules are delayed." In fact only the high-risk obligations moved, pushed past 2027. The transparency rules — chatbot disclosure, deepfake labeling — switch on August 2 exactly as planned.

The law follows the output, not the head office. Like GDPR, a Korean or US company with no EU entity is covered the moment its AI reaches a user in Europe. An earlier date matters more than most coverage suggests: companies that sign the Code of Practice by July 22 gain a presumption of conformity, shifting the burden of proof to regulators rather than themselves.

Underneath every transparency duty sits one question — where did this output come from — and the data governance needed to answer it. That is the same provenance and quality work Pebblous has built into DataClinic. With 78% of affected organizations yet to start, the next six weeks are less a compliance cost than a head start in EU procurement.

## Facebook (EN)

Plenty of people exhaled in May, when the news read as Europe easing its AI rules.

Look at the AI Omnibus a little longer, though, and only the heavy obligations moved: the high-risk systems used in hiring or credit scoring.

The quieter rules kept their date. From August 2, a chatbot in the EU has to admit it isn't a person, and a deepfake has to carry a mark saying a machine made it.

What stays with me is how little the law cares about your risk tier, or your address. One ordinary support bot, one AI image in a campaign, and you are in scope. A company in Seoul or in Austin is covered the moment its output reaches a user in Europe. Anyone who lived through GDPR will recognize the shape of it.

"But we don't even have an EU entity." We have already watched that sentence stop working once.

Six weeks remain. I read the figure, 78% of affected organizations yet to begin, less as a threat than as open space. For a company that has kept track of where its data comes from, August 2 may not be a new wall, but one more line on a floor already laid.
