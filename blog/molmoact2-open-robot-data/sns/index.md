비영리 연구기관 Ai2가 로봇 파운데이션 모델 MolmoAct 2를 공개하면서, 모델 가중치만이 아니라 720시간 분량의 양손 조작 학습 데이터와 코드, 평가 절차를 통째로 열었습니다. NVIDIA GR00T, Physical Intelligence π0.5, Google DeepMind Gemini Robotics가 가중치는 일부 풀면서도 학습 데이터는 닫아둔 것과 정반대의 선택입니다.

데이터까지 열리면 누구든 결과를 재현하고, 약점을 진단하고, 자기 환경에 맞게 다시 학습시킬 수 있습니다. 가중치가 완성된 요리라면 데이터는 레시피와 재료입니다. 실제로 MolmoAct 2는 실세계 로봇팔에서 작업별 미세조정 없이 평균 87.1% 성공률을 기록하며 폐쇄형 모델을 여러 지표에서 앞섰습니다.

물론 비영리 기관이라 가능했던 결정이기도 합니다. 영리 기업에게 데이터 공개는 경쟁 우위의 포기지만, Ai2에게는 투명성과 재현성이라는 존재 이유에 가깝습니다.

다음 로봇 모델을 평가할 질문은 한 칸 옮겨갈 것입니다. 가중치를 공개했는가가 아니라, 그 가중치를 만든 데이터까지 공개했는가.

## LinkedIn (EN)

The real contest in robot AI quietly shifted. It's no longer about who builds the better model, but about who opens the data.

The nonprofit Allen Institute for AI (Ai2) released its robot foundation model MolmoAct 2 and opened not just the weights, but 720 hours of bimanual manipulation training data, the code, and the full evaluation procedure. That's the opposite of what NVIDIA's GR00T, Physical Intelligence's π0.5, and Google DeepMind's Gemini Robotics have done — ship some weights, keep the data closed.

Open the data and anyone can reproduce the results, diagnose the weak spots, and retrain for their own setting. Weights are the finished dish; data is the recipe and the ingredients. In practice MolmoAct 2 hit 87.1% average success on a real robot arm with no per-task fine-tuning, beating closed models on several benchmarks.

It helps that Ai2 is a nonprofit. For a commercial lab, opening the data means handing rivals the same starting line; for Ai2, transparency and reproducibility are closer to the reason it exists.

The question we ask of the next robot model will move one notch over. Not "did they open the weights," but "did they open the data that made those weights."

## Facebook (EN)

I keep running into the phrase "open-source robot model" lately.

But peel back what "open" usually means, and you find a single file: the model weights. What the model learned, and from what data, stays closed.

Weights are the finished dish. Data is the recipe and the ingredients. With only the dish in hand, you can't cook the same thing twice.

That's why MolmoAct 2, released by the nonprofit Ai2, stopped me. It opened not just the model but 720 hours of bimanual manipulation data, the training code, and the evaluation procedure. The first robot model anyone can rerun, trace when it fails, and reshape for their own setting.

One question lingers. "Why did this choice come only from a nonprofit?" For a commercial lab, opening the data means putting a rival on the same starting line. The same act is a cost for one party and a purpose for another, and that asymmetry explains why models this open have been so rare.

As the real moat in robot AI shifts from architecture to data, who holds that data and who opens it becomes a question of data sovereignty. The place where Pebblous has spent years looking at AI-Ready data is not far from here.
