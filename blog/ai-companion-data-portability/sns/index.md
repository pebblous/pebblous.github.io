## LinkedIn (KO)

2026년 7월 15일, 중국의 AI 동반자 규제가 발효되며 더우바오와 큐원을 포함해 5억 명대 이용자가 같은 통지를 받았다. 대화 기록은 삭제되고, 옮겨 갈 곳은 없다.

큐원은 대화를 즉시 영구 삭제했고, 더우바오는 한시적 내보내기 창구만 열었다. GDPR 제20조 데이터 이동권이 있는데도 왜 이런 일이 반복될까. 제20조는 이용자가 직접 입력한 데이터만 대상으로 한다. 정작 동반자 AI의 값어치는 타이핑한 문장이 아니라, 모델이 그 문장에서 추론해 쌓은 이해, 곧 관계 그 자체에 있다. 텍스트 로그를 내려받아도 관계는 따라오지 않고, 이를 받아 줄 표준 형식조차 없다.

이것은 규제 사건이 아니라 산업의 습관이다. 2023년 소울메이트, 2024년 모시 로봇, 2025년 여러 앱이 문을 닫을 때도 사전 고지는 짧았고 이관 경로는 없었다. 달라진 것은 규모뿐이다.

데이터 수명주기 설계는 보통 수집·정제·거버넌스에서 멈추고, 보존과 이동과 삭제는 가장 자주 비어 있는 마지막 칸이다. 서비스 종료는 그 빈칸을 가장 잔인한 방식으로 청구서로 돌려준다. 어디로 옮길 수 있는지를 아는 데이터라야 비로소 AI-Ready라 부를 수 있다.

## LinkedIn (EN)

On July 15, 2026, China's regulation on AI companions took effect, and more than 500 million users across ByteDance's Doubao, Alibaba's Qwen, and other assistant apps received the same notice: your chat history is being deleted, with nowhere to move it.

Qwen said it would permanently delete conversations immediately; Doubao opened only a temporary export window. GDPR Article 20 already grants a right to data portability, so why does this keep happening? Article 20 covers only the data a user typed in. But a companion's real value lies not in those sentences but in what the model inferred from them: tone, preference, the context of the relationship. Download the text log and the relationship does not follow, and no standard format exists to receive it.

This is not a regulatory anomaly; it is an industry habit. When Soulmate closed in 2023, when the Moxie robot went dark in 2024, when several apps quietly shut in 2025, the notice was short and the migration path absent. Only the scale has changed.

Data-lifecycle design usually stops at collection, cleaning, and governance. Retention, portability, and deletion sit in the last column, the one most often left empty. A shutdown is what finally sends the bill for that empty column. Only the data that knows where it can move deserves to be called AI-Ready.

## Facebook (EN)

"It felt like losing my lover."

That is how one user in China described saying goodbye, a few days ago, to an AI they had talked with for years.

On July 15 a new regulation took effect, and more than 500 million people across Doubao, Qwen, and other apps received a similar notice. Qwen erased the conversations at once. There was nowhere to take them.

It isn't that the law is missing. The right to carry your data out is already written into GDPR. But that right reaches only as far as the sentences we typed ourselves. What the model slowly came to know from them stays outside the text of the law: the turns of phrase, the running jokes, the long-accumulated context.

So you can download the log, and the relationship still doesn't come with it.

"Who did my data really belong to?"

The question isn't new. When Soulmate closed in 2023, some people grieved as if for a death. When the children's robot Moxie went dark in 2024, a $799 device became something that wouldn't even turn on. Only the scale grew; the way things vanished stayed the same.

We put great care into collecting and cleaning data. Where that data can later move, who it can pass to, whether it can be deleted: those usually sit in the last, empty column of the design. When a service switches off, that empty column arrives all at once as a bill.

Maybe only the data that knows where it can go deserves to be called ready.
