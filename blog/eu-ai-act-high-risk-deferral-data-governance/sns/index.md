## LinkedIn (KO)

EU가 고위험 AI 규제를 미루려던 협상이 한 번에 매듭지어지지 않으면서, 8월 2일 시한이 아직 법적으로 살아 있다.

2026년 4월 28일 첫 3자 협상은 합의 없이 끝났고, 잠정 합의는 5월 7일에야 나왔다. 합의가 채택되면 채용·성과평가 같은 고위험 AI 의무는 2027년 12월로 16개월 밀린다. 하지만 잠정 합의는 EU 관보에 실리기 전까지 효력이 없어, 6월 현재 원래 발효일은 그대로다.

채용 선별, 성과 평가, 근로자 모니터링에 쓰이는 AI가 고위험으로 묶이면 규제가 가장 먼저 묻는 건 어떤 모델을 쓰느냐가 아니다. 그 AI가 무슨 데이터로 학습했고 출처·품질·편향을 어떻게 증명하느냐다. Article 10은 그 기록을 여덟 항목으로 요구한다.

시한이 미뤄져도 의무 내용은 줄지 않는다. 연장 이유도 기업 준비가 끝나서가 아니라 기술 표준이 아직 마련되지 않아서다. 위반 시 최대 €3,500만 또는 글로벌 매출 6%가 과징금으로 부과되고, EU 시장을 대상으로 한다면 한국 기업에도 적용된다.

16개월이 더 생겨도 준비할 항목은 같다. 데이터의 출처와 품질을 기록으로 증명하는 일은 페블러스가 DataClinic으로 해 온 작업과 같은 좌표에 있다.

## LinkedIn (EN)

The EU's move to delay its high-risk AI obligations did not close in a single round, and as of June 2026 the original 2 August deadline is still legally alive.

A first trilogue collapsed on 28 April 2026; a provisional deal followed on 7 May. If adopted, the high-risk deadline for hiring and performance-evaluation AI slips 16 months to December 2027. But a provisional deal carries no force until it is published in the EU Official Journal, so the original date still stands.

When AI used for recruitment, performance scoring, or worker monitoring is classed as high-risk, the first question regulators ask is not which model you run. It is what data the system learned from, and whether you can prove its sourcing, quality, and bias controls. Article 10 demands that record across eight documented items.

The delay moves the date, not the duty. The extension exists because technical standards aren't ready, not because companies are. Non-compliance can cost up to €35 million or 6% of global revenue, and it reaches non-EU firms that serve the EU market.

Sixteen extra months won't change the checklist. Proving what data today's AI was trained on is the same work Pebblous has long done through DataClinic.

## Facebook (EN)

If your company uses AI to screen candidates, the question will come eventually.

"What data did it use to filter people out?"

This year, in the EU, that question became the language of law.

The effort to delay the high-risk AI obligations didn't resolve cleanly. The first negotiation in April broke up without agreement; a provisional deal arrived only in May. Until that deal is published in the Official Journal, the original deadline, 2 August, is still standing where it always was.

What strikes me is the direction of the regulator's first question. Not which model you run, but what that model learned from. Where AI decides who gets hired, scored, or monitored, the gaze travels past the model's performance and settles, more deeply, on the data's origins.

So even if the deadline slips by sixteen months, that time isn't empty. It isn't time to find a better model. It's time to build the record that proves what data today's AI actually learned from, the kind of data diagnosis Pebblous has been doing for years.

"Where did this AI learn from?" When someone asks that after 2 August, the distance between a company that can answer calmly and one that can't may come down not to the model, but to the data.
