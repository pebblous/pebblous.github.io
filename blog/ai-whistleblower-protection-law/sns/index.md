## LinkedIn (EN)

For the first time, U.S. lawmakers are moving to protect workers who report AI wrongdoing, in a way no employment contract can override. The bipartisan AI Whistleblower Protection Act (S.1792), introduced by Senators Chuck Grassley and Chris Coons, would let employees and contractors report AI-related violations to regulators and Congress even when bound by an NDA or arbitration clause. Whistleblowers wouldn't need to prove a violation, only a reasonable, good-faith belief, and those who face retaliation could recover their jobs, double back pay, and legal fees. A 269-page discussion draft released in June, the Great American AI Act, goes further, asking frontier developers above $500M in revenue to disclose what data they trained on and what risks they shipped. The shift worth noting is where accountability now begins. Earlier rules policed outputs: a biased hiring decision, a fraudulent deepfake. These bills protect the people who saw the data and model decisions before any output existed, turning the everyday work of choosing training data and signing off on deployment into legal testimony. Data governance is quietly becoming a labor-rights question.

## Facebook (KO)

AI가 무언가 잘못됐을 때, 그것을 가장 먼저 아는 사람은 누구일까요.

대개는 학습 데이터를 고른 사람, 평가 결과를 읽은 사람, '배포해도 된다'고 서명한 사람입니다.

2024년, OpenAI를 떠난 직원들은 회사가 내민 계약서 앞에서 침묵을 택해야 했습니다. 비판하지 않겠다는 조항에 서명하지 않으면 수백만 달러의 스톡옵션을 잃는 구조였습니다.

그래서 미국 연방이 움직이기 시작했습니다. 상원의 AI 내부고발자 보호법은 NDA가 있어도 AI 위반을 규제 당국에 알릴 수 있게 하고, 보복당한 신고자에게 복직과 두 배의 임금을 돌려줍니다.

이 법안에서 오래 남은 건 보호의 방향이었습니다. 지금까지 규제는 차별적 판정이나 딥페이크처럼 이미 나와버린 결과를 단속했습니다. 이 법은 그 이전으로, 데이터와 모델 결정을 본 사람에게로 시선을 옮깁니다.

AI 책임의 첫 증거가 시스템 로그가 아니라 그 결정을 목격한 사람이라면, 데이터 계보를 기록하는 일은 더 이상 엔지니어링 위생의 문제만은 아닐 겁니다. 누가 무엇을 보고 무엇을 결정했는지가 남아 있을 때, 그 기록은 책임의 증거이면서 동시에 그 사람을 지키는 방패가 됩니다.

## Facebook (EN)

When an AI system goes wrong, who knows it first?

Usually it's the person who chose the training data, who read the evaluation results, who signed off on the release.

In 2024, employees leaving OpenAI faced a quiet choice. Sign a non-disparagement clause, or forfeit millions in vested equity. Many signed, and stayed silent.

Now Congress is moving. The AI Whistleblower Protection Act would let those workers report AI violations to regulators even under an NDA, and give anyone who is retaliated against their job back, along with double pay.

What stayed with me was the direction of that protection. Older rules watched the output: a discriminatory decision, a convincing deepfake. This one looks earlier, to the people who saw the data and model decisions that produced it.

If the first evidence of AI accountability is not a log file but a person who witnessed the decision, then recording data lineage stops being mere engineering hygiene. When there is a record of who saw what and decided what, that record is both proof of responsibility and a shield for the one who raised a hand.

That is the same place our work at Pebblous keeps arriving at: keeping a careful account of where data came from, and what was decided along the way.
