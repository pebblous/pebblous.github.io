1976년 4색 정리는 프로그램과 검증 목록이 공개돼 누구나 재현할 수 있었다. 이번에는 결과는 검증돼도 과정은 다시 밟을 수 없다. AI가 새 지식을 만들 수 있다는 것과 그 지식을 검증 가능하게 만드는 일은 별개다. 결과의 신뢰는 모델 성능이 아니라 출처·과정·재현성이라는 흔적에서 온다.

## LinkedIn (EN)

An 80-year-old math conjecture has fallen, and not to a purpose-built theorem prover but to OpenAI's general-purpose reasoning model.

The 1946 Erdős unit distance conjecture held that the maximum number of point pairs exactly distance 1 apart in the plane grows almost linearly. The model reached into algebraic number theory and built a configuration that grows faster, disproving it. Fields Medalist Tim Gowers said he would recommend the proof to the Annals of Mathematics without hesitation.

But the nine mathematicians who verified it never saw the model's raw output. They reviewed an edited summary of its reasoning, then rewrote the proof in terms a human could follow. Whether the same prompt reproduces the same proof is something no one outside OpenAI can confirm.

When computers first cracked the 1976 four-color theorem, the program and its checklist were public, so anyone could reproduce the result. This time the result is verifiable, but the path to it is not. Creating new knowledge and making that knowledge verifiable are two different problems. Trust comes not from model performance but from a traceable record of source, process, and reproducibility.

And a quiet question lingers. "If you gave the same model the same prompt, would this proof appear again?" No one outside OpenAI can answer that with confidence.
