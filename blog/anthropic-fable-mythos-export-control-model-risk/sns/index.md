## LinkedIn (EN)

A three-day-old AI model vanished worldwide on a single government order.

On the evening of June 12, 2026, the U.S. Commerce Department directed Anthropic to block "foreign nationals" from Claude Fable 5. But a cloud API has no way to verify a user's nationality in real time. To stay compliant, Anthropic pulled the model globally, U.S. users included. It is the first time export controls have been applied to a specific frontier AI model.

Stripe halted a codebase migration, Mozilla paused a vulnerability review, and enterprises that had wired Fable 5 into Amazon Bedrock on launch day lost it the next morning. No one received advance notice. No terms were violated and no bills were unpaid. A model companies were legally paying for disappeared overnight, on a regulatory call they could not influence.

The model is rented infrastructure, and infrastructure can be cut off from outside. What remains to carry a company through is the data it owns and has verified itself.

## Facebook (EN)

Can a tool you pay for, and use without breaking a single rule, simply disappear one night?

On the evening of June 12, a model only three days old went dark across the world. Anthropic's Fable 5.

The cause was a single directive from the U.S. Commerce Department. Its target was "foreign nationals," but a model running in the cloud has no way of telling, in real time, who is foreign. So everyone lost it, U.S. users included.

I keep thinking about the people who had to stop working that night.

Stripe's codebase migration, halted. Mozilla's vulnerability review, paused. Teams that had wired the model in on launch day came back the next morning to a screen where it no longer was. No warning, and no fault of their own.

We may have come to treat a model like a faucet that is always on. Always there, pay for what you use. What this week quietly showed is that the handle sits outside the house, in a hand we cannot reach.

So one question stays with me. "If the model is gone tomorrow, can we begin again with our own data?"

A model is borrowed, and what is borrowed can be recalled. What stays behind to carry a company forward is the data it has built and verified itself. That is the ground Pebblous has spent its time on.
