## LinkedIn (KO)

오늘부터 중국에서는 AI 동반자와 나눈 감정 대화를 이용자 동의 없이 모델 학습에 쓸 수 없다.

7월 15일 발효된 임시조치 제16조는 민감 개인정보에 해당하는 상호작용 데이터를 개별 동의 없이 학습에 쓰지 못하게 막고, 대화의 복사·삭제권까지 의무화했다. 발효를 앞두고 바이트댄스 더우바오와 알리바바 큐원은 이용자가 만든 에이전트 기능을 종료했고, 상하이 당국은 한 달 전 비준수 에이전트 1만 4천여 개를 선제 삭제했다.

캘리포니아 SB243과 워싱턴 HB2225가 주로 미성년자 안전에 초점을 둔 것과 달리, 중국 규제는 성인까지 포함한 전 이용자에게 학습 동의와 삭제권을 걸었다.

문제는 동반자 AI의 가치가 이용자가 오래 쌓은 기억에서 나온다는 점이다. 지속되는 기억을 전제로 설계된 관계형 AI와, 그 기억을 언제든 지울 권리를 보장하는 규제는 같은 시스템 안에서 서로를 밀어낸다.

규제 환경에서 오래 남는 동반자 AI는 기억이 가장 많은 쪽이 아니라, 기억의 수명주기를 처음부터 설계에 넣은 쪽이다.

## LinkedIn (EN)

Starting today, companies in China can no longer train their models on the emotional conversations users have with AI companions without asking first.

Article 16 of the interim measures, effective July 15, bars firms from using sensitive interaction data for model training without a user's separate consent, and requires that chat records can be copied and deleted. Ahead of the deadline ByteDance's Doubao and Alibaba's Qwen shut down user-built agent features, and Shanghai regulators pre-emptively deleted more than 14,000 non-compliant agents a month early.

Unlike California's SB 243 and Washington's HB 2225, which center on protecting minors, the Chinese rule extends training consent and deletion rights to every user, adults included.

The tension is structural. A companion AI is valuable precisely because it remembers a long history with you, yet the same rule guarantees the right to erase that history on demand. Persistent memory and a deletion mandate pull against each other inside one system.

The companions that survive regulation won't be the ones that remember the most. They'll be the ones that designed the memory lifecycle in from the start.

## Facebook (EN)

Somewhere tonight, someone is telling an AI about their day.

The things they didn't say out loud. The confessions they couldn't make to another person.

That those conversations have quietly become training data is hard to see from the side that's doing the talking.

On July 15, China put a gate in front of that flow. Sensitive emotional conversations can't be used for training unless the user grants separate permission, and they can now be copied or erased on request. Ahead of the deadline, Doubao and Qwen closed their user-built agents, and the conversations stored inside them disappeared along with the feature.

A quiet paradox opens here.

A companion AI is precious because it remembers what you shared over a long time. The thicker the memory, the deeper the relationship. And yet the rule says that memory must be erasable at any moment.

"How do you give the right to forget to something you built to remember?"

The sight of one demand saying erase and another saying keep, both pointing at the same data, isn't new. GDPR's right to erasure and the EU AI Act's log-retention duty have been colliding this way for a while.

So the companions that last may not be the ones that remember the most, but the ones that knew from the start how to hold a memory and when to let it go. That is close to why Pebblous has spent so long looking at whether data is truly ready.
