투게더 AI가 지난 7월 1일 8억 달러를 조달하며 밸류에이션 83억 달러에 올랐습니다. 사우디 아람코의 벤처 부문이 라운드를 이끌었고 엔비디아도 들어왔습니다. 16개월 전 밸류가 33억 달러였으니 2.5배 넘게 뛴 값입니다.

이유는 돈의 방향이 바뀌었기 때문입니다. 오픈소스 모델이 최고 폐쇄형 모델을 성능에서 거의 따라잡자, 기업들은 값비싼 API 대신 훨씬 싼 오픈소스로 옮겨갔습니다. 그러자 자금은 모델을 만드는 쪽이 아니라, 그 모델을 실제로 돌릴 GPU와 전력으로 흘렀습니다.

그런데 그 인프라마저 값이 무너지고 있습니다. 엔비디아 H100의 임대가는 고점 대비 60~75% 내렸습니다. 모델을 상품화한 힘이 인프라 계층에서도 똑같이 작동하기 시작한 것입니다.

값이 무너지는 계층은 결국 누구나 살 수 있습니다. 같은 오픈 모델을 같은 네오클라우드에서 비슷한 값에 돌리게 되면, 마지막까지 복제되지 않는 것은 각자가 그 위에 얹는 데이터의 품질입니다.

## LinkedIn (EN)

A company that rents out GPUs just got an $8.3B price tag. It builds no AI models of its own.

Together AI raised $800M on July 1, lifting its valuation to $8.3B. Aramco's venture arm led the round, and Nvidia joined. Sixteen months ago the company was worth $3.3B, so this marks a 2.5x jump.

The reason is that the money has changed direction. As open-source models closed nearly all of the performance gap with the best closed models, enterprises moved off expensive APIs and onto far cheaper open weights. The capital followed, flowing not into the labs that build models but into the GPUs and power that run them.

Yet even that infrastructure is losing its price. Hourly rental rates for Nvidia's H100 have fallen 60 to 75% from their peak. The same force that commoditized the model layer has started working on the infrastructure layer.

Whatever loses its price can eventually be bought by anyone. Once rivals run the same open model on the same neocloud at a similar cost, the one thing left uncopied is the quality of the data each of them puts on top.

## Facebook (EN)

A few days ago I read that a company building no models of its own had just been valued at $8.3 billion.

Together AI. It stockpiles GPUs and rents them out by the hour to anyone who wants to run AI.

For a while, models were the whole story. Whoever held the best model was the winner.

Then open-source models nearly caught up to the best closed ones, and enterprises started moving to the far cheaper open weights. The money followed, but not to the labs that build models. It flowed to the GPUs and the power that run them. Together AI's price tag sits exactly on that spot.

What comes next is the strange part. Even that infrastructure is losing its price, quickly. H100 rentals are down 60 to 75% from their peak. The same force that made models cheap has started working on the machines that run them.

So I kept circling one question. "If the moat keeps flowing from whatever loses its price toward whatever holds it, where does it finally settle?"

From the vantage of someone who has worked with data for a long time, the clue is there. The moment everyone runs the same open model at a similar cost, what separates the results is the data each of them lays on top. How accurate it is. How current. How much of the domain it actually holds. That is the reason Pebblous has stayed with data quality for so long.

The cheaper the model gets, the more the data remains. Together AI's valuation may be nothing more than a single snapshot of a moat still in motion.
