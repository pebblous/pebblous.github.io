## LinkedIn (EN)

An AI agent's quality is only as good as the data it sees.

Israeli startup Jedify closed a $24M Series A this week, led by Norwest with Snowflake Ventures joining as a strategic investor. What Jedify sells is neither a model nor a GPU. It builds a "context graph" that weaves an enterprise's scattered data into a single semantic layer agents can actually read. That layer had been the last unfilled line item in AI infrastructure budgets — even as Q1 2026 saw $300B in global venture funding pile into the sector, 81% of it destined for AI.

The pressure driving demand is structural. Agents don't query data the way people do. A single agentic workflow can hit a retrieval layer hundreds of times in seconds. Systems designed for human-scale queries crack under that load. Enterprise intent to adopt hybrid retrieval tripled from 10% to 33% in a single quarter — a clean signal that pure RAG is reaching its limit.

The harder truth is this: even a well-built context layer cannot repair what it reflects. If the underlying data is stale, inconsistent, or governed loosely, the agent fails in exactly the same way. The context layer bet is ultimately a bet on data quality and governance.

The missing line item in your 2026 AI budget is context. Before the tooling, audit the data it will read.

## Facebook (EN)

"Why does the agent keep saying wrong things with such confidence?"

That's the question echoing through enterprise teams after their first real agentic deployments.

The easy answer is "that's just how LLMs work." But the evidence points somewhere else.

Most agent hallucinations trace back not to the model, but to the data the model was given.

Duplicates with conflicting values. The same customer split across systems under different IDs. Information that was accurate yesterday but stale today.

Jedify, an Israeli startup, just closed a $24M Series A.

What they build is a "context graph" — a layer that weaves an enterprise's scattered data into a structured semantic model that agents can actually read and trust.

In Q1 2026, $300B in venture capital flooded into startups, 81% of it aimed at AI. But the context layer was the last place that money reached.

Here is the uncomfortable part.

No matter how well-designed the context layer is, if the underlying data is stale, inconsistent, or weakly governed, the agent fails in exactly the same way.

The honest answer to "will this tool eliminate hallucinations?" is:

"Only if your data is ready."

The old principle — data before tooling — turns out to be the most practical advice in the agentic era.

The AI-Ready data problem that Pebblous has been working on through DataClinic is the same foundation that investors are now discovering under the name "context layer."
