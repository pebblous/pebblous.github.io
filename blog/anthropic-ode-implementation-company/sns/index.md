랩들은 이 병목을 '데이터'가 아니라 '인재 부족'이라 부른다. 그런데 그 비싼 엔지니어가 반년씩 현장에서 하는 일의 상당 부분은 데이터 정제, 계보 문서화, 품질 규칙 수립이다. 조 단위가 배포로 흐르는 동안에도 가장 앞선 마이크로소프트의 Copilot 주간 실사용률은 1% 언저리에 머문다.

배포 인력이 사람 손으로 메우는 것은 결국 기업 데이터의 준비도다. 다음 예산이 모델 라이선스로 향하기 전에 먼저 물어야 할 질문이 여기 있다.

Ode's own chief technology officer described model choice as "similar to picking a programming language when you build software" — a model-company executive reducing his own product to one ingredient in the system.

The labs call this bottleneck a "talent shortage," not "data." Yet much of what those expensive engineers do on the floor for months is data cleaning, lineage documentation, and quality rules. Even Microsoft, the furthest ahead, sees Copilot weekly active usage sit near 1%.

What deployment teams patch by hand is enterprise data readiness. That is the question to ask before the next budget line goes to a model license.

## Facebook (EN)

I read last week that a company built to make AI models had just spent $1.5 billion, and that what it bought was not a bigger model, but people.

The company is Ode, the "implementation firm" Anthropic helped launch.

What stayed with me was not the size of the check but a sentence from its chief technology officer. Asked about model choice, he said it was "similar to picking a programming language when you build software." An executive at a model company, quietly filing his own product under "one ingredient."

So where does the real work go?

The labs call that place a "talent shortage." But look closely at what those expensive engineers do on an enterprise floor for months, and much of it is tying scattered data together, recording where it came from and how it changed, and writing the rules that keep it clean. They just never called it "data."

Even as billions flow toward deployment, Microsoft's Copilot, the furthest-ahead player, sits near 1% weekly usage. That gap does not close by adding more people. For AI to earn its keep, an organization's data has to be in a state it can actually read.

Turning that months-long, by-hand work into something repeatable and measurable is what Pebblous has been doing as AI-Ready Data: tying data together by relationship, recording its lineage, setting quality rules.

Before the next budget line goes to a model license, maybe there is only one question worth asking. Is our data in a shape that would swallow a deployment engineer's half-year?
