## LinkedIn (EN)

An AI that matches physics-based models on next week's weather quietly paints a colder world when asked about the distant future.

In a 2026 Geophysical Research Letters study, FourCastNet, Pangu-Weather, and the climate emulator ACE2 all returned forecasts resembling a climate 15 to 20 years earlier than the period they were meant to predict. Over the warming-heavy US East, that gap stretched to 20 to 30 years.

The cause lies not in the models' cleverness but in what they were trained on. Their training data captures a mostly cooler past, and when the models meet an unfamiliar warmer future, they fall back on the average they know. Tellingly, a simple statistical baseline delivered a more consistent mean climate than the neural models did.

A high benchmark score means a model does well inside the range it was measured on. It does not mean the model understands the system. The distribution of the training data is the model's ceiling, and the real risk always grows quietly outside it.

'AI가 기상청을 이겼다'는 문장을 지난 몇 년 동안 여러 번 보았습니다.

며칠 앞 날씨를, 슈퍼컴퓨터도 없이 몇 초 만에. 그 성적은 진짜였습니다.

그런데 같은 모델에게 먼 미래의 기후를 물었더니, 답이 한쪽으로 조용히 기울었습니다. 실제보다 차가운 세상. 예보하려던 시점보다 15년, 20년쯤 이른 기후를 닮은 그림이었습니다.

이상한 것은 방향이었습니다. 틀리더라도 무작위로 틀리지 않고, 늘 더 차가운 쪽으로만 틀렸다는 것.

왜 그럴까 따라가 보면 답은 모델 바깥에 있었습니다. 세 모델이 배운 데이터는 대부분 지금보다 서늘했던 과거였고, 배운 적 없는 더운 미래를 만나자 모델은 익숙한 과거로 되돌아갔습니다. '더 정교한 AI가 더 나은 답을 준다'는 믿음도, 단순한 통계 베이스라인이 오히려 더 일관된 기후를 내놓은 자리에서 잠깐 멈춰 섰습니다.

그래서 요즘 자꾸 되묻게 됩니다. 벤치마크에서 이긴다는 것은 어디까지를 이긴 걸까요? 우리가 가진 데이터는, 우리가 정말 예측하고 싶은 미래를 담고 있을까요?

무엇으로 배웠는가가, 무엇을 영영 모르는가를 정합니다. 데이터가 얼마나 새로운가와, 그 데이터가 예측하려는 세상을 얼마나 대표하는가는 다른 질문입니다. 페블러스가 데이터를 들여다볼 때 늘 함께 묻는 자리도 여기입니다.

## Facebook (EN)

"AI beat the national weather service." I've read that sentence more than once over the past few years.

A few days out, in seconds, without a supercomputer. And the scores were real.

Then someone asked the same models about the distant future, and the answers leaned quietly to one side. A colder world. A picture that looked like the climate of fifteen, twenty years before the moment it was meant to forecast.

What unsettled me was the direction. The models didn't miss at random. They missed cold, every time.

The reason sat outside the model. What these systems learned was a past that ran cooler than today, and when they met a warmer future they had never seen, they returned to the past they knew. Even the belief that a more sophisticated AI gives a better answer paused for a moment, in the place where a plain statistical baseline held a steadier climate than the neural models did.

So I keep coming back to two questions. When a model wins on a benchmark, how far does that win actually reach? And does the data we hold really contain the future we most want to predict?

What you learn from decides what you can never see. Freshness and representativeness are not the same question. That second one is where Pebblous keeps looking. The full piece is here: https://blog.pebblous.ai/blog/ai-climate-model-cold-bias/en/
