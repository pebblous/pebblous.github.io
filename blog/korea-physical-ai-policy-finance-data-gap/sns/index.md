7월 1일 금융위원회와 산업통상부가 로봇·미래차·반도체를 포함한 6개 산업에 국민성장펀드 정책금융을 배정한다고 발표했다. 1호 승인은 LS전선 해저케이블 공장 증설이고, 이는 6월에 예고된 10년 1,350조 원 삼축 전략의 첫 집행이다. 돈은 대체로 공장과 설비, 만질 수 있는 자산으로 흐른다.

문제는 학습 데이터의 성질이다. 디지털 AI는 웹에 이미 쌓인 텍스트와 이미지를 긁어 학습하지만, 로봇이 물체를 쥘 때의 힘이나 미끄러짐 같은 물리 데이터는 접촉이 일어나는 순간에만 생성된다. 크롤링으로는 확보되지 않는다.

참여 기업 목록에도, 정책 문서에도 공장과 로봇 본체는 보인다. 그런데 그 기계를 실제로 움직이게 할 데이터를 수집하고 정제하는 몫은 예산 어디에도 명시돼 있지 않다.

하드웨어를 세우는 것이 첫 챕터였다면, 휴머노이드 점유율을 1%에서 20%로 끌어올린다는 목표를 실현할 데이터는 다음 챕터가 답해야 한다.

## LinkedIn (EN)

South Korea has staked ₩16 trillion ($10.3B) on physical AI this year. Read the budget through a data lens, and one line is missing.

On July 1, the Financial Services Commission and the Ministry of Trade earmarked National Growth Fund financing for six industries, from robotics to autos to semiconductors. The first approval went to an LS Cable undersea-cable plant, the opening move of the ₩1,350 trillion, ten-year triple-axis strategy announced in late June. The money flows into factories and equipment, into assets you can touch.

The catch is the nature of the training data. Digital AI learns from text and images already sitting on the web. The physical data a robot needs, the grip force, the moment of slip, the torque on a joint, is generated only at the instant of contact. It cannot be crawled.

The list of participating firms names plants and robot bodies. Nowhere does the budget name a line item for collecting and curating the data that would make those machines actually work.

If building the hardware was the first chapter, the data to hit a jump from 1% to 20% humanoid market share is what the next chapter has to answer.

## Facebook (EN)

"From 1% to 20% humanoid market share."

When I first read the target the Korean government set, my mind went somewhere else. What, exactly, would those robots watch and learn from?

On July 1, financial regulators earmarked ₩16 trillion ($10.3B) for six industries, from robotics to semiconductors. The first project was an LS Cable undersea-cable plant, and behind it stands a much larger plan worth ₩1,350 trillion over ten years. The scale and the direction are both clear.

But go down the list of what this money buys, and almost everything is an asset you can touch. Factories. Production lines. Chip fabs. The body of a robot. And yet what makes that body move is data.

Digital AI grows up reading the text and images already piled on the web. Physical AI does not. The grip force, the moment of slip, the load on a joint, all of it is born only at the instant of contact, and none of it sits waiting anywhere.

So the budget leaves one question open.

"Who collects the data these machines will learn from, and where?"

This isn't a verdict on the policy. It's just that if the hardware was chapter one, the data to move it is what chapter two has to answer. That is the same place Pebblous has spent a long time looking at the quality of physical data.
